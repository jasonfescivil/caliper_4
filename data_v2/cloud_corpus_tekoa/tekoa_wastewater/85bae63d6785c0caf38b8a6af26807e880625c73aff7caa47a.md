Decennial Census Counts of Population for the State, Counties, Cities and Towns
Washington State Office of Financial Management, Forecasting and Research Division
The tables contained herein provide decennial census population counts for the state, counties, and cities.
The following symbols are used denote special circumstances regarding a census count:
$   Indicates a federal correction to a federal census count.  Census corrections indicated from 1970 forward.
"*   Indicates a state certified special census count rather than federal census count.  State special censuses indicated from
      1980 forward."
The census data have varied to a certain extent from one census to another.  The date for the 1890 and 1900 censuses was June 1; for 1910, April 15; for 1920, January 1; and for 1930 through 2020, April 1.
For counties, the formation date in many cases is the date when they were created by the Oregon Territorial Legislature.  Clark County was originally created as Vancouver District in 1845.  However, the first board of county government was first organized in 1850 after the 1849 Oregon Territorial Legislature changed the name to Clark from Vancouver.  The formation date for Spokane County is the date when it regained separate county status after having been merged with Stevens County.
For cities, year of incorporation indicates date of first incorporation.  Several cities and towns have been reincorporated after disincorporation.  Great care has been taken to authenticate the date of original incorporation with the most reliable sources.  It will be noted that there are certain discrepancies between the present compilation and those prepared by the Office of the Secretary of State and by the Association of Washington Cities.
