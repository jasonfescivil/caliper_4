National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) Permit Requirements

Overview:
The NPDES permit program, created by the Clean Water Act (CWA) Section 402, controls water pollution by regulating point sources that discharge pollutants into waters of the United States.

Key Requirements:

1. BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand) Limits:
   - Secondary treatment standards: 30 mg/L (30-day average)
   - 45 mg/L (7-day average)
   - 85% removal efficiency required

2. TSS (Total Suspended Solids) Limits:
   - Secondary treatment: 30 mg/L (30-day average)
   - 45 mg/L (7-day average)
   - 85% removal efficiency required

3. pH Requirements:
   - Must be maintained between 6.0 and 9.0 standard units

4. Monitoring and Reporting:
   - Discharge Monitoring Reports (DMRs) must be submitted monthly
   - Flow must be monitored continuously
   - Sampling must follow EPA-approved methods

5. Permit Application Timeline:
   - New facilities: Apply at least 180 days before discharge
   - Existing facilities: Renewal applications due 180 days before expiration
   - Permits valid for maximum of 5 years

6. Technology-Based Effluent Limits:
   - Best Practicable Control Technology (BPT)
   - Best Available Technology Economically Achievable (BAT)
   - Best Conventional Pollutant Control Technology (BCT)

7. Water Quality-Based Effluent Limits:
   - Must ensure compliance with state water quality standards
   - Total Maximum Daily Loads (TMDLs) may impose stricter limits
