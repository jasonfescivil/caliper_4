Washington Administrative Code
Chapter 173-308 WAC - Biosolids Management

WAC 173-308-160 - Biosolids pollutant limits

(1) Biosolids sold or given away in a bag or other container or biosolids that are land applied must meet:
(a) Ceiling concentration limits in Table 1; and
(b) One of the following:
    (i) Table 3 pollutant concentration limits; or
    (ii) Cumulative pollutant loading rates in Table 2 and annual pollutant loading rates in Table 4.

Table 1 - Ceiling Concentration Limits (same as federal)
Table 3 - Pollutant Concentration Limits
Pollutant | Monthly average (mg/kg dry weight)
Arsenic | 41
Cadmium | 39
Copper | 1,500
Lead | 300
Mercury | 17
Nickel | 420
Selenium | 36
Zinc | 2,800

WAC 173-308-170 - Pathogen reduction

(1) All biosolids that are land applied must be treated by a process to significantly reduce pathogens (PSRP) or a process to further reduce pathogens (PFRP).

(2) Class A biosolids - Pathogen density requirements
(a) The density of fecal coliform must be less than 1,000 MPN per gram of total solids (dry weight), or
(b) The density of Salmonella sp. bacteria must be less than 3 MPN per 4 grams of total solids (dry weight).

(3) Class B biosolids - Pathogen density requirements
The density of fecal coliform must be less than 2,000,000 MPN per gram or CFU per gram of total solids (dry weight).

WAC 173-308-210 - Sampling and analysis

(1) Representative samples - Samples must be collected and analyzed in accordance with the methods referenced in WAC 173-308-120.

(2) Frequency of monitoring
(a) Facilities that prepare less than 290 dry metric tons per year must monitor at least once per year.
(b) Facilities that prepare 290 to 1,500 dry metric tons per year must monitor at least once per quarter.
(c) Facilities that prepare 1,500 to 15,000 dry metric tons per year must monitor at least once per 60 days.
(d) Facilities that prepare 15,000 dry metric tons or more per year must monitor at least once per month.
