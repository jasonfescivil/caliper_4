### Draft Section
The project will apply Class B biosolids to non‑public access farmland at agronomic nitrogen rate in accordance with federal and state biosolids rules. Class B pathogen reduction is demonstrated by fecal coliform density below 2,000,000 MPN per gram total solids [2], and vector attraction reduction will be achieved by at least 38% volatile solids reduction [1][2]. Public access will be restricted for 12 months after application as required for Class B materials [2].

Site controls include setbacks of at least 100 ft to surface waters and 300 ft to occupied dwellings. While a 100‑ft surface water buffer is consistent with typical permit conditions, the dwelling setback is often established by local permits; therefore, we will confirm with the permitting authority during design [3]. Seasonal restrictions will prohibit land application on frozen or snow‑covered ground where runoff could occur [3].

Short supporting quotes:
- “Class B biosolids… may be land applied where access is controlled and site restrictions are followed.” [1, p. 118]
- “Reduce volatile solids by at least 38 percent.” [1, p. 130]
- “Fecal coliform less than 2,000,000 MPN per gram…” [2, §503.32(b)(2)]
- “Do not apply to frozen or snow‑covered ground if runoff is likely.” [3, p. 5‑10]
