1) Establish planning horizon and design period                                                                                                                                                                                     
- Use the jurisdiction’s adopted plans and expected pipe life: “planning horizon…as little as 10 years…as much as 50 years” and consider “saturation or build-out” where appropriate [13, p.54].                                    
- Forecasts are applied at start and end of design period for flow sizing [1, p.55]. Quote: “done for the beginning and end of the design period” [1, p.55].                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
2) Confirm regulatory planning framework and submittals                                                                                                                                                                             
- General sewer plan and engineering report must include population trends and projections: “estimated future population for the stated design period” and describe method and planning-agency concurrence [11, p.1; 6, p.6].       
- State guidance requires using official data: “Provide Office of Financial Management (OFM) or other population data…Include population projections for the…design period” consistent with local land use agencies [5, p.48].      
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
3) Select data sources and coordinate with planners/demographers                                                                                                                                                                    
- Prefer land-use-based inputs over pure trend extrapolation: “use data on population and residential land use…from the jurisdiction’s planning department” (general plan, zoning, development plans) [1, p.55].                    
- Keep plans current and sequence growth: “planners…inform the engineer as to which parts…will be developed before others” and use “most current versions” [1, p.55].                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
4) Structure the forecasting workflow (per WEF summary)                                                                                                                                                                             
- Follow a stepwise procedure: “Step 1: Determine planning horizon… Step 2: Forecast population or number of dwelling units… Step 3: Determine average unit flows… Step 4: Develop average flows” and then “Develop design and peak and minimum flows” [4, p.51].                                                                                                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
5) Residential population forecast (DU-based)                                                                                                                                                                                       
- Use DU/acre and persons/DU by density class. Example densities: “≤4 DU/acre…3.0 persons/DU; 4–10…2.8; >10…2.5 persons/DU” [1, p.55].                                                                                              
- Adjust for development timing and vacancy: “% developed at end of planning horizon” and “Population forecasts may also be adjusted by a housing vacancy rate” [8, p.56; 1, p.55].                                                 
- Include transients and group quarters: “include transients…group quarters…seasonal-use facilities” and, if needed, “convert to equivalent full-time residents by using multipliers” [1, p.55].                                    
- Apply at subarea key points for hydraulics: “divide into subareas…most downstream point…is the key point” [1, p.55].                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
6) Nonresidential projections (employment-based)                                                                                                                                                                                    
- Forecast land uses/employment from plans: “forecast the land use and/or number of employees” using “general plan…zoning…development plans” [7, p.56].                                                                             
- Use FARs and floor area per employee to estimate employees; apply by category (office, retail, institutional, etc.) [7, p.56; 15, p.57].                                                                                          
- Compute beginning- and end-of-period estimates for each sewer subarea and key point [15, p.57].                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
7) Translate population to wastewater flows                                                                                                                                                                                         
- Residential per-capita benchmarks: average daily indoor use “600 to 650 L/d (160 to 170 gpd)” for a “median household size of 2.6 persons” [2, p.99].                                                                             
- For larger household sizes: “use…225 L/capita/d (60 gpd/capita)” [2, p.99].                                                                                                                                                       
- For mixed areas, estimate nonresidential flows individually “for each source using appropriate criteria or metered water use” [2, p.99].                                                                                          
- Recognize small-population variability; consider equalization or modularity when flows vary: “flows vary substantially…consideration should be given to flow equalization…modular designs” [2, p.99].                             
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
8) Account for infiltration and inflow (I/I)                                                                                                                                                                                        
- Design must include extraneous flows: estimate “current and future BWF and RDII to insure that sewers have adequate conveyance capacities” [21, p.14].                                                                            
- Definitions to separate components: BSF, GWI, inflow, ADW, peaking, etc. (e.g., “Base Sanitary Flow…excludes infiltration and inflow”) [38, p.3].                                                                                 
- Even for small systems: “Infiltration and inflow should not be overlooked…identify and repair or eliminate…or…handle the excess water” [2, p.99].                                                                                 
- Rationale: “Excess storm and groundwater…robs the system of its valuable capacity” and drives overflows [10, p.7].                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
9) Develop design, peak, and minimum flows from forecasts                                                                                                                                                                           
- After average flows by subarea are developed, proceed to peaks/minima for sizing: “Develop design and peak and minimum flows” following the forecasting steps [4, p.51].                                                          
- Use beginning- and end-of-period conditions for facility capacity planning; apply at “many key points” in the system [1, p.55; 15, p.57].                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
10) Address uncertainty and scenario planning                                                                                                                                                                                       
- Treat forecasts as uncertain; manage via alternatives: “Forecasts…are not statements of fact…Two or more planning scenarios” and sensitivity analyses are recommended [12, p.75].                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
11) Documentation in engineering submittals (design basis)                                                                                                                                                                          
- Engineering reports must state present and future “quantity and quality of wastewater” and discuss I/I and variability [6, p.6; 18, p.7].                                                                                         
- Ensure consistency with approved general sewer plan; reports must be “sufficiently complete” to support design without “substantial changes” [5, p.48].                                                                           
- Environmental review linkages: SEPA/NEPA/SERP may apply to planning documents [30, p.39; 26, p.41].                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
12) Example tabular structure (to implement the method)                                                                                                                                                                             
- Use a table like WEF Table 3-2 with columns: “Area (acres), DU/acre, No. of DUs, Persons/DU, % Developed…, Total Population” [8, p.56].                                                                                           
- Note: include transient facilities (e.g., “Resort Hotel…convert to equivalent full-time residents”) [8, p.56; 1, p.55].                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
Key takeaways                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
- Base projections on current land use/zoning and development phasing, not only trends [1, p.55].                                                                                                                                   
- Forecast both residential (DU-based) and nonresidential (employment/FAR-based) contributors [7, p.56; 15, p.57].                                                                                                                  
- Convert population to average flows with per-capita rates, then address peaking and I/I for design capacity [2, p.99; 4, p.51; 21, p.14].                                                                                         
- Document methods and assumptions to meet WAC engineering report and plan requirements with planning-agency concurrence [11, p.1; 5, p.48].
