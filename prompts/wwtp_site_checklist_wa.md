# Prompt 1 — Generate the “WWTP Site Consideration Checklist” (Washington-specific)

Create a comprehensive, Washington-specific “WWTP Site Consideration Checklist” for evaluating a proposed wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) site. Align the checklist with Ecology’s Orange Book and key Washington regulations (WAC 173-240, 173-221, 173-201A, 173-200, 173-308), plus Shoreline Management Act (RCW 90.58), SEPA (RCW 43.21C), CWA 401/404, WDFW HPA, and typical local Critical Areas Ordinances (CAO).

Deliverables (in this order):

1. A brief purpose statement (2–3 sentences).
2. A grouped checklist—one Markdown table per group—using exactly these headings (add as many sub-rows as needed):

   - A. Land Use & Property
   - B. Floodplain, Shoreline & Surface Waters
   - C. Groundwater & Critical Aquifer Recharge Areas (CARA)
   - D. Wetlands, Streams & Critical Areas
   - E. Geotechnical & Seismic
   - F. Receiving Water & Outfall (NPDES/Mixing Zone/TMDLs)
   - G. Reclaimed Water & Land Application Feasibility
   - H. Odor, Noise, Public Receptors & Buffers
   - I. Utilities, Access, Power & Bypass/Cutover
   - J. Collection System Integration & Hydraulics
   - K. Permitting & Environmental Review (SEPA/NEPA/Local)
   - L. Construction, Phasing, Risks & Resilience (Climate/Fire/Snow)
   - M. Biosolids Handling & Haul Logistics

3. Each table must contain these columns:

   - Checklist Item / Question (what must be verified)
   - Why it Matters (one concise sentence)
   - Evidence/Data Needed (surveys, studies, maps, calcs, monitoring)
   - Key Standards/References (e.g., “WAC 173-240-060(3)(e)”, “FEMA FIRM”, “RCW 90.58 SMA”, “40 CFR 122/131/503”, “WAC 173-308”)
   - Go/No-Go / Flag (Yes/No/Needs Mitigation)
   - Where to Document in Report (chapter/section; e.g., “Receiving Water”)

Content guidance:

- Include actionable checks: FEMA floodway vs 100-yr floodplain; shoreline jurisdiction/conditional use; 7Q10 & mixing zone; 303(d)/TMDL status; CARA susceptibility; wetland class, buffers & mitigation; liquefaction/slope hazards; odor/noise buffers to nearest receptors; power/standby; haul routes; cutover/bypass plans; climate resiliency.
- Keep tone neutral—it’s a template to assess any candidate site in Washington.
- Return only the checklist (no extra narrative) in copy-pasteable Markdown.
