This flow consists of the true sanitary flow (Qs) generated by residential, commercial, and institutional users, combined with groundwater infiltration (QGWI) that may seep into the collection system through pipe defects, joints, or manholes, especially when groundwater levels are seasonally high. It explicitly excludes significant, direct commercial or industrial process discharges that are not part of the baseline sanitary load, if any. 
The assumption of "no additional inflow" during dry weather conditions is critical for isolating the sanitary and groundwater infiltration components. Inflow is primarily associated with direct stormwater entry into the sanitary sewer system, commonly from sources such as improperly connected sump pumps, roof leaders, foundation drains, or cross-connections with storm drains, all of which are activated by precipitation events. By analyzing flow during periods without rainfall, these direct inflow contributions are minimized or eliminated, allowing for a more accurate assessment of the baseline wastewater generation and persistent infiltration. <<check>>
