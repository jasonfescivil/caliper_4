Wet Weather Flow (WWF) is qualitatively defined as the significantly increased and often rapidly fluctuating flow observed at the WWTP during and immediately following a rainfall event. This flow includes the normal ADWF components (sanitary flow and baseline groundwater infiltration) plus the additional, often substantial, volume contributed by RDII. The magnitude, timing (how quickly the flow responds to rain), and duration of the WWF response depend on numerous factors.
