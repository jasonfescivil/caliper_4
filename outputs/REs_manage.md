Workflow to prepare a Residuals (Biosolids) Management Plan within an Engineering Report

1) Confirm planning basis and scope
- Verify the engineering report is consistent with an up-to-date, Ecology-approved general sewer plan; if not, include needed general-plan elements per WAC 173-240-060(1) so any engineer can proceed to design without substantial changes [6, p.45]. Ensure the report is sufficiently complete to support plans and specifications [5, p.37; 6, p.45].

2) Define residuals planning objectives and regulatory framework
- State that the residuals plan will ensure compliance with applicable state and federal requirements, including WAC 173-308 (biosolids), 40 CFR 503, Ecology’s Minimal Functional Standards, and local permits. If proposing landfill disposal, include justification that no feasible beneficial use options exist and show compliance with 40 CFR 258 and WAC 173-351 [1, p.54].

3) Establish current and future solids production and characteristics
- Quantify expected solids quantities and quality for the design period; include a solids mass balance for the treatment plant to support evaluation of sludge treatment and wastewater treatment alternatives [1, p.54].
- Identify existing and potential industrial contributors and their waste characteristics insofar as they may affect solids quality; consider future industrial expansion [4, p.6].

4) Develop and screen residuals treatment and end-use/disposal alternatives
- Identify feasible sludge treatment options at the plant and relate each to potential biosolids beneficial-use options, including regional biosolids options. Include no-action for context and any nonstructural measures if relevant [1, p.54; 3, p.50].
- Ensure each alternative can meet WAC 173-308/40 CFR 503 and other applicable requirements [1, p.54].

5) Evaluate alternatives and select a preferred approach
- Compare alternatives using criteria such as environmental impact, present worth cost-effectiveness, technical complexity and availability of certified operators, project risk, and legal/regulatory barriers; document reasons options are unacceptable where applicable [3, p.50].
- Present a ranking of residual solids handling alternatives, identify the preferred alternative, and list actions necessary for implementation. Also present reasons for not selecting other alternatives [1, p.54].

6) Cost development
- Provide capital and O&M costs for the life of the project; present total annual cost and present worth, and describe assessment methods. Include the engineer’s best opinion of probable final costs, methods used, and expected accuracy [1, p.54].

7) Staffing and testing
- Identify staffing levels, qualifications, specialization, and laboratory/testing needs specific to solids treatment, handling, and biosolids program compliance; ensure staffing aligns with facility classification and includes an operator in responsible charge per WAC 173-230 cross-reference in the staffing discussion [1, p.54].

8) Provisions for future needs
- Discuss how residuals facilities and end-use capacity will accommodate growth under high/medium/low profiles; assess expansion potential, siting constraints, and regional options over the planning horizon [1, p.54].

9) Implementation plan and schedule
- Detail permits/approvals, phasing, procurement, and key milestones needed to implement the preferred residuals management alternative [1, p.54; 5, p.37].

10) Environmental review and funding alignment (if applicable)
- If seeking Ecology-administered funding, include a cost-effectiveness analysis of the selected alternative and complete environmental review consistent with Ecology’s EPA‑approved SERP (leveraging SEPA to satisfy most NEPA-like elements and public participation requirements) [5, p.37; 7, p.41].

11) Documentation and submittal
- Compile supporting data, calculations (including solids mass balance), and decision rationale so that subsequent plans/specs can be developed without substantial changes. Prepare, seal, and submit the engineering report under a Washington-licensed professional engineer; coordinate paper/electronic submittal per Ecology’s practices [5, p.37; 6, p.45].
