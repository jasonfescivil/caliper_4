Narrative (county-based approach, compliance, and use in design)
Applying OFM Medium Whitman County growth rates directly to Tekoa’s base-year population follows the documented Method 1 in the plan (Pop = Prevpop × (1+%Δ)) and aligns the city forecast with county-adopted medium-series growth, providing consistency with regional planning expectations. The facility plan explicitly applies county-wide OFM projections to Tekoa and sets planning horizons to 2030 and 2045, matching the 20‑year requirement in WAC 173‑240‑060(3)(b) for estimating future loads over the design period [1], [6], [14]. This approach is conservative for a small rural city: county medium growth for Whitman County (outside the high-growth Pullman core) has been modest, and using it for Tekoa tends to avoid overestimation of future populations in the absence of documented major developments. Key assumptions include the base-year Tekoa population of 820 from the planning spreadsheet, the OFM-based household size of 2.4 persons per household, and defining one Residential ERU per household at 190 gpd [12]. Limitations: the provided index lacks the full OFM Medium series for 2025, 2035, 2040, and 2045 and contains no Growth Management allocation/assumption tables, so only 2030 could be calculated from the primary medium-series data shown [5], [6], [8], [11], [24]. Once the complete OFM Medium rates are supplied, the same method yields Tekoa’s 5-year increments through 2045. The ERU outputs translate directly to design flows by multiplying ERUs by 190 gpd to estimate average annual daily flow (AADF) and then applying peaking and I/I allowances per the Orange Book and facility plan conventions [12], [14].
