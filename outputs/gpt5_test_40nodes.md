Key G1 requirements for engineering reports (Washington State Department of Ecology, Criteria for Sewage Works Design, Chapter G1) are:

- Purpose and scope
  - Provide a comprehensive, site‑specific analysis and preliminary design basis so Ecology can determine compliance with applicable requirements and, if relevant, issue a discharge permit. Reports must be sufficiently detailed that any engineer can develop plans and specifications without substantial changes [G1‑4.1.1, p.43; G1‑2.5.1, p.37] [9] [4].
  - “Sufficiently complete” means no reevaluation of selected unit processes and only minor unit‑sizing changes; any “substantial change” (process, size, criteria, performance, environmental impacts, or cost) requires an amendment [Table G1‑1 explaining WAC 173‑240‑060(2), p.48] [19].

- When required (and exceptions)
  - Required for projects constructing or modifying domestic wastewater facilities (structures, equipment, or processes to collect, convey, treat, reclaim, or dispose) and for projects discharging to waters of the state, except large on‑site systems regulated by DOH [G1‑4.1.2, p.45] [1].
  - Normally not required for sewer line extensions or pump stations if they conform to an Ecology‑approved general sewer plan; exceptions include proposals with overflows/bypasses or that discharge to overloaded facilities, and Ecology may require reports for funded projects [G1‑4.1.2, p.45; WAC 173‑240‑060(1)] [1] [18].

- Content (aligns with WAC 173‑240‑060)
  - If an up‑to‑date general sewer plan is not on file, include the appropriate WAC 173‑240‑050 general plan items in the report; otherwise demonstrate consistency with the approved plan [Table G1‑1 on 060(1), p.48] [19].
  - Include at minimum: owner/authorized representative info; project description with location and service area maps; present and projected flows and loads (including industrial); required degree of treatment; receiving water description and water quality compliance; proposed process and alternatives analysis; design data and sizing for each unit; site discussion (including 25‑ and 100‑year flood relationships); flow diagram and hydraulic profile; I/I, overflows and proposed corrections; industrial waste provisions/pretreatment; outfall/disposal analysis; sludge management; provisions for future needs; staffing and testing; capital and O&M cost estimates and financing; statements on compliance with water quality management plans and SEPA/NEPA [WAC 173‑240‑060(3)(a)–(r)] [18] [20]. 
  - Ecology’s G1 guidance elaborates data expectations (e.g., at least one year of flow/loading data; use accredited labs and calibrated meters; projections consistent with approved plans) [Table G1‑1 on 060(3)(c), p.48–49] [19].

- Process and timing
  - Sequence: Ecology‑approved general sewer plan → engineering report approval → plans and specifications approval → construction (with construction QA plan, O&M manual, and completion declaration) [G1‑2.2, Figure G1‑1, p.33–35; WAC 173‑240‑030(3)] [16] [31].
  - Coordinate early with Ecology regional engineers; submit a draft for preliminary review (electronic or paper) [G1‑4.1.3, p.45–46] [1].
  - Submittal at least 60 days before desired approval; Ecology strives to review within that timeframe but complexity/workload may extend it [WAC 173‑240‑030(2); G1‑4.1.3, p.45–46] [31] [1].
  - Final reports must be prepared under the supervision of, and sealed/signed by, a Washington‑licensed professional engineer; submit at least one paper and one electronic copy (electronic submittals must meet G1‑2.6 e‑signature/seal standards) [G1‑4.1.3, p.46; G1‑2.6, p.39–40] [1] [17].

- Environmental review and funding
  - Adoption of an engineering report is an “action” requiring SEPA compliance; NEPA/SERP may also apply for federally funded projects. Ecology’s SERP (approved by EPA) relies on SEPA plus expanded public participation for CWSRF projects [G1‑2.7, p.39–41; G1‑1.3.1, p.18; G1‑2.5.1, p.37] [17] [2] [4] [15].
  - For Ecology‑funded projects, the report must include a SERP‑compliant environmental review and a cost‑effectiveness analysis; consult current Water Quality Combined Funding Program guidelines [G1‑2.5.1, p.37; G1‑2.8, p.41] [4] [15].
  - For projects seeking federal funding, Ecology recognizes the USDA‑RD/EPA/HUD/IHS preliminary engineering report (PER) template as best practice [G1‑4.1.1, p.43–44] [9].

- Review standards
  - Ecology reviews for compliance with permit needs and RCW 90.48/90.54 policies and for reasonable consistency with the Orange Book and other accepted references [G1 intro, p.12; WAC 173‑240‑040] [8] [30].
