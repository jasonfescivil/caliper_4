Receiving Water Analysis & Water Quality Standards (WQS) Compliance Workflow (Engineering Report format) – Town WWTP (<1,000 population)

Purpose and scope
- Demonstrate compliance with WAC 173-240-060(e): describe the receiving water, identify applicable WQS, and show how WQS will be met outside any applicable dilution/mixing zone; prepare content complete enough for plans/specifications without substantial changes [2, p.6].
- Apply Ecology’s receiving-water data, mixing zone, pollutant evaluation, and antidegradation expectations, using credible data and analyses suitable for a small municipal discharge [1, p.50; 4, p.49].

1) Project and receiving-water description
- Identify discharge location(s): name of receiving water; river mile and/or latitude/longitude; waterway segment number; township/range; outfall location map; water quality classification [1, p.50; 2, p.6].
- Summarize water system proximity if relevant to siting and protection (wells, reservoirs, treatment facilities), and note existing/potential discharge locations [2, p.6].

Deliverables:
- Location and service-area maps; receiving water classification summary [2, p.6; 1, p.50].

2) Compile and curate receiving-water data
- Gather existing data from: Ecology’s EIM, EPA’s WQX; acceptable sources such as USGS, NOAA, FERC, and any site-specific monitoring done for this report. Include site-specific data in an appendix [1, p.50].
- Use credible data: laboratory results from Ecology-accredited labs; flow data from meters with documented calibration; identify influent/effluent sampling locations, sample types, and process return streams [4, p.49].

Deliverables:
- Data inventory and QA summary; appendices with datasets and methods [1, p.50; 4, p.49].

3) Characterize receiving-water hydraulics for mixing and compliance
- Freshwater: determine 7Q10 flow; describe channel width, slope, bed roughness (Manning’s n), velocity, and local features influencing flow near the outfall at 7Q10. Ecology uses this to evaluate any proposed mixing zone [1, p.50].
- Saltwater/estuaries: determine outfall depth at MLLW; develop current direction/velocity profiles (ebb and flood); vertical profiles for salinity, density, temperature from discharge elevation to surface at MLLW. Ecology uses this to evaluate allowable mixing-zone size/shape [1, p.50].

Deliverables:
- Low-flow/hydrodynamic characterization and profiles; input set for dilution/mixing analysis [1, p.50].

4) Define effluent flows and loads for current and future conditions
- Establish present and projected waste loads (normally 20-year planning horizon) based on population/land use, existing and anticipated industrial sources, and effects of water conservation and climate change on I/I if applicable. Identify combined sewer/CSO interactions if present [4, p.49].
- Use credible, calibrated flow meters and accredited lab data; document sampling locations and sample types for influent/effluent and internal returns [4, p.49].

Deliverables:
- Flow/load summary (current and design), with variability characterization and data QA/QC [4, p.49].

5) Identify applicable WQS and permitting drivers
- Compile applicable numeric and narrative criteria in Chapter 173-201A WAC for: conventional pollutants (BOD5, TSS, pH, dissolved oxygen, temperature), pathogens (Enterococci/E. coli/Fecal Coliform, as applicable), and toxics commonly present in municipal discharges (e.g., ammonia, chlorine) [4, p.49].
- If receiving water is on the 303(d) list, include listed parameters; align design values with TMDL waste load allocations where established; if none, demonstrate no reasonable potential to cause or contribute to a WQS violation for impaired parameters [4, p.49].

Deliverables:
- WQS applicability matrix and target endpoints for compliance assessment [4, p.49].

6) Mixing-zone/dilution analysis and antidegradation
- Using the hydraulic characterizations, model near-field/far-field dilution as needed to predict concentrations at the boundary of any authorized chronic mixing zone; show how WQS will be met outside the zone [2, p.6; 1, p.50].
- Demonstrate whether the discharge will cause a measurable change at the chronic mixing-zone boundary. Use Ecology’s thresholds:
  - Temperature increase ≥0.3 C
  - DO decrease ≥0.2 mg/L
  - Bacteria increase ≥2 cfu
  - pH change ≥0.1 units
  - Turbidity increase ≥0.5 NTU
  - Any detectable increase in toxics/radioactive substances (most sensitive 40 CFR 136 method) [4, p.49].
- Consult regional Ecology staff on the level of analysis needed for antidegradation under WAC 173-201A-300 to -330 [4, p.49].

Deliverables:
- Mixing-zone modeling inputs/outputs; compliance demonstration at zone boundary; antidegradation consultation notes [1, p.50; 4, p.49].

7) Biological and use-support evaluations
- Evaluate effects of toxic chemicals on migratory fish (e.g., potential barriers to migration) and broader designated uses for pollutants reasonably expected in the effluent [1, p.50].
- Saltwater: assess microbial (bacteria/virus) impacts on shellfish beds; reference Guidance Document #19 in the 2015 National Shellfish Sanitation Program Model Ordinance for prohibited area sizing near WWTPs [1, p.50].
- If discharge is to groundwater, address minimum requirements of a hydrogeologic study per Ecology’s Implementation Guidance for Ground Water Quality Standards [1, p.50].

Deliverables:
- Designated-use impact assessment; shellfish/microbial analysis (if marine); hydrogeologic study summary (if groundwater) [1, p.50].

8) Whole effluent toxicity (WET) considerations
- Identify chemicals that may be present and aquatic species present in the receiving water; discuss need/frequency of biomonitoring WET testing to address synergistic effects; note WET rule Chapter 173-205 WAC [1, p.50].

Deliverables:
- WET screening and proposed monitoring plan, if warranted [1, p.50].

9) Compliance strategy and linkage to treatment performance
- Connect predicted receiving-water concentrations to required effluent limits or performance targets to ensure WQS compliance outside the mixing zone; consider current permit or likely future permit requirements and potential for future regulatory tightening over the facility life [4, p.49].
- For small systems, document feasible operational and treatment reliability features that ensure consistent compliance with identified limiting criteria [4, p.49].

Deliverables:
- Summary of limiting pollutants/criteria and corresponding effluent performance requirements; permit linkage [4, p.49].

10) Documentation, submittal, and appendices
- Include: current permit (or anticipated requirements for new discharges); receiving-water data summaries; models and inputs; maps; sampling and QA/QC documentation; and all site-specific monitoring data in an appendix [1, p.50; 4, p.49].
- Ensure the engineering report is sufficiently complete for plans/specs without substantial changes; submit required copies to Ecology per WAC 173-240-060(2) [2, p.6].

Checklist (tie-back to WAC 173-240-060)
- 3(e) Receiving water description, applicable WQS, and how WQS will be met outside any applicable dilution zone [2, p.6; 1, p.50].
- 3(d) Degree of treatment required analysis supporting WQS compliance, including 303(d)/TMDL and measurable change determination; antidegradation consultation [4, p.49].
- Credible data and calibrated flow measurements; sampling locations/types documented [4, p.49].
- Marine/shellfish and groundwater-specific evaluations where applicable [1, p.50].
