### Objective and Purpose of Engineering Reports [1][4][9][19]
Under Washington's General Engineering Requirements (G1), an engineering report is defined as a comprehensive document that examines the engineering and administrative aspects of a domestic wastewater facility (WAC 173-240-020(6)) [1][4][21]. It must provide site-specific evaluations of alternatives, preliminary engineering details, and rationale for design criteria to allow Ecology to assess compliance with water quality standards, permit conditions, and environmental protection under Chapters 90.48, 90.52, and 90.54 RCW [1][3][9]. The report ensures facilities can be designed, constructed, operated, and maintained to meet effluent limitations and protect public health and waters of the state [3][9]. It must be "sufficiently complete" so plans and specifications can be developed without substantial changes, defined as no reevaluation of unit processes or more than minor unit-sizing modifications; substantial changes (e.g., treatment process, facility size, or environmental impacts) require report amendments [1][4][19]. For federal funding (e.g., EPA's CWSRF), the report aligns with 40 CFR 35 facility plans and includes cost-effectiveness analysis and SERP environmental review [1][4][15]. Ecology recognizes USDA-RD, EPA, HUD, and IHS interagency PER templates as best practices [1][9].

### Projects Requiring Submittal [1][4][9][18][21]
Engineering reports are required for all domestic wastewater facilities involving collection, treatment, reclamation, or disposal (WAC 173-240-020(5)), except large on-site sewage systems (LOSS) regulated under Chapter 70.118B RCW by DOH [1]. Submittal is mandatory for:
- New or expanded treatment plants, complex projects, or those with overflows, bypasses, or discharges to overloaded systems [1].
- Projects seeking state/federal funding (e.g., Ecology's Water Quality Financial Assistance) [1][4].
- Sewer line extensions or pump stations only if they deviate from an approved general sewer plan (WAC 173-240-020(13), 173-240-030(5)) or include non-conforming elements [1][4][18].
No report is typically needed for routine extensions/pump stations in approved general sewer plans with referenced design criteria [1]. For reclaimed water, reports must also comply with WAC 173-219-210 and may integrate with WAC 173-240-060 or 246-272B WAC [7][10][22]. Industrial reports follow WAC 173-240-130 [12][23].

### Content Requirements (WAC 173-240-060) [1][4][18][19][20]
Reports must be prepared under a Washington-licensed professional engineer's supervision, sealed/signed per Chapter 18.43 RCW, and include three copies (or one electronic/paper for drafts/finals per G1-2.6) [1][4][13][18]. If no up-to-date general sewer plan exists (WAC 173-240-050), the report incorporates its elements [1][18][19]. Minimum contents [18][19][20]:
- **Owner/Representative Details**: Name, address, phone/email of owner and authorized representative with contract authority [18][19].
- **Project Description**: Location maps of service area, facilities, and vicinity; "where, what, why" rationale tied to general sewer plan needs [18][19].
- **Wastewater Quantity/Quality**: Current/future (20-year) flows/loadings (e.g., BOD5, TSS, industrial wastes, I/I, diurnal/seasonal variations); ≥1 year of accredited lab/flow meter data; sources (domestic/commercial/industrial percentages) [4][18][19].
- **Treatment/Disposal Needs**: Degree required based on permits, receiving water, wastewater strength; description of proposed processes, alternatives evaluated/rejected; basic design data/sizing calculations; expected efficiencies/effluent character [18][19][20].
- **Receiving Water Analysis**: Description, standards met outside dilution zones; detailed outfall analysis [18][20].
- **Site Evaluation**: Available sites' pros/cons; flood risks (25/100-year); proximity to residences/developments [18][20].
- **Flow Diagrams/Hydraulics**: Unit layout, effluent discharge, hydraulic profile [18][20].
- **I/I, Overflows/Bypasses**: Problems, proposed corrections/controls [18][20].
- **Industrial/Sludge Provisions**: Pretreatment for significant sources; sludge disposal methods/alternatives [18][20].
- **Geohydrologic (Land Application)**: Soils/permeability; groundwater depth/movement; water balance; effects with other facilities; reserve areas [18][20].
- **Future/Staffing**: Provisions for expansion; staffing/testing requirements [18][20].
- **Costs/Financing**: Capital/O&M estimates; debt service per service; assessment methods; total annual/present worth costs [18][19][20].
- **Compliance Statements**: SEPA/NEPA; water quality management plans (federal/state); SERP if funded [4][15][18][19][20].
For reclaimed water, add: projected quantity/quality meeting WAC 173-219-390 Table 3 uses; treatment processes/reliability; cross-connection controls (WAC 173-219-310); source water characteristics (BOD5, TSS, nitrates); private utility capacity if applicable [7][10][22]. Pilot studies may be required for novel treatments [34].

### Submission, Review, and Approval Procedures (G1-4.1.3) [1][4][9][13][18]
- **Early Coordination**: Meet Ecology regional engineers pre-submittal for complex/funded projects; discuss finance, timelines, permitting [1][9].
- **Timing**: Submit ≥60 days before desired approval (WAC 173-240-030(2)); account for 60-90+ day reviews based on complexity/workload [1][9][13][18].
- **Format**: One electronic/paper draft for preliminary review; final: ≥1 paper +1 electronic (signed/stamped per G1-2.6, flattened PDF preferred) [1][4][9][17]. Electronic stamps must prevent easy copying [17].
- **Review Standards**: Ecology assesses for WAC 173-240-060 compliance, Orange Book consistency (e.g., CSWD manual), good engineering practices, AKART, and environmental protection [1][3][4][12][13]. Nonlead agencies (e.g., DOH) may review [10][13].
- **Approval**: Regional offices approve; provisional for re-ratings (G1-5.3.2) with 12+ month monitoring [11]. Revisions needed if >2 years pass without construction [18][21].
- **Exceptions**: Delegated to locals (G1-5.2) if criteria met; no PE seal for some industrial mods [21][23]. CSO projects add sediment impacts/source control (WAC 173-245-040) [5].

Patterns/Insights: G1 requirements integrate with broader planning (e.g., general sewer plans per WAC 173-240-050 [16][29][30]) and environmental reviews (SEPA/NEPA/SERP [15][17][19]), emphasizing iterative, phased processes to avoid duplication (e.g., combined reports for reclaimed water [7][22]). Funding ties reports to federal standards, promoting cost-effectiveness and public participation [1][4][15]. Connections to WAC 173-240 ensure alignment with NPDES/State Waste Discharge permits (Chapters 173-220/216 WAC [26]), prioritizing containment of wastes and I/I exclusion [36].
