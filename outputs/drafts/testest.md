Workflow for lift-station design flows, peaking, pump sizing, and wet well volume (WA Ecology compliant)

1) Define service basis and design horizon
- Establish current and 20‑year forecast service population for the tributary area; design the station’s firm capacity on the peak hourly design flow, considering projected growth and collection‑system improvements, and verify operation under low‑flow conditions [C2‑1.2.1, p.260; C2‑1.2.2, p.260–261 in 1].

2) Establish average daily flow (ADF)
- Use local per‑capita wastewater flow criteria or metered water‑use data per Ecology; alternative methods (e.g., water consumption, actual measured flows) are acceptable with documentation [C1‑3.3.1, p.209 in 5].
- If local values are unavailable, a defensible planning value is 60 gal/capita/day (225 L/capita·d) for populations exceeding the 2.6‑person median household size [5.3.1, p.99 in 8].
- Example (town of 400; no growth assumed for illustration): ADF = 400 × 60 = 24,000 gpd = 16.7 gpm. If a 20‑year forecast is higher, scale ADF accordingly [C2‑1.2.1, p.260 in 1].

3) Account for infiltration/inflow (I/I)
- New systems: per‑capita criteria are intended to cover normal I/I [C1‑3.3.3, p.209 in 5].
- Existing systems or unfavorable conditions: add an I/I allowance based on measured data to the greatest extent possible [C1‑3.3.3, p.209 in 5].

4) Determine peak hourly design flow (PHF)
- Select the peaking factor from Ecology’s Figure C1‑1 based on contributing population; do not use less than 2.5 [C1‑3.3.2, p.209 in 5].
- For 0.4 thousand population, interpolate between 0.3k (~3.8) and 0.5k (~3.6); use ≈3.7 (minimum 2.5) [C1‑3.3.2, p.209 in 5].
- Example: PHF = ADF × 3.7 = 24,000 × 3.7 = 88,800 gpd = 61.7 gpm (≈62 gpm). If I/I was added, apply the same peaking factor to the I/I‑adjusted ADF.

5) Establish firm capacity and operating envelope
- The station’s firm capacity (with the largest pump out of service) shall be equal to or greater than the peak hourly design flow; also evaluate minimum flows and low‑flow operation [C2‑1.2.1, p.260; C2‑1.2.3, p.260 in 1].
- System hydraulics: evaluate highest and lowest head conditions for new/aged pipes and for all pump combinations; use conservative loss coefficients and avoid long‑term operation outside manufacturers’ recommended range [C2‑1.2.2, p.260–261; p.262 in 2].

6) Select the number of pumps (redundancy and cycling)
- Requirement: With the largest pump out of order, the station must pass the peak design flow, and the number of pumps should correlate to wet‑well sizing to avoid excessive short‑cycling [C2‑1.2.3, p.260 in 1; p.262 in 2].
- Constant‑speed stations often determine pump count based on achieving minimum scour velocity in the force main [C2‑1.2.3, p.262 in 2].
- Small‑town examples for PHF ≈ 62 gpm:
  - Duplex (2 equal pumps): To meet “largest out,” one remaining pump must pass PHF; select each pump for ≈62 gpm at design head.
  - Triplex (3 equal pumps): Two pumps in service must pass PHF; select each pump for ≈31 gpm at design head so two in parallel meet ≈62 gpm.

7) Preliminary pump selection
- Use solids‑handling sewage pumps capable of passing ≥3‑inch solids with 4‑inch or larger suction/discharge (unless grinder/STEP exceptions apply) [C2‑1.2.4, p.262 in 2].
- Develop and overlay the system curve(s) on manufacturer pump curves for all relevant configurations (1, 2, … pumps on line; min/max heads) to confirm acceptable operating points [C2‑1.2.2, p.260–261 in 1]. Document in the O&M manual [C2‑1.7, p.266 in 3].

8) Wet‑well sizing (constant‑speed)
- Objective: acceptable intake hydraulics, adequate volume to limit pump starts, sufficient depth for controls, and minimize solids deposition [C2‑1.2.5, p.262–263 in 2].
- Minimum active volume between pump on/off for constant‑speed pumps: V = t × Q / 4, where t = minimum time between starts (minutes) per motor/pump manufacturer and utility policy, and Q = pump capacity (gpm) [C2‑1.2.5, p.262–263 in 2].
- Example (illustrative only; select t per manufacturer/owner):
  - Duplex case (one pump capacity Q ≈ 62 gpm): If t = 10 minutes, V = 10 × 62 / 4 = 155 gallons between on/off.
  - Triplex case (one pump capacity Q ≈ 31 gpm): If t = 10 minutes, V = 10 × 31 / 4 = 77.5 gallons between on/off.
- Add volumes for required submergence, inlet hydraulics, control band separation, and any prescribed emergency storage; use trench/hopper geometry with ≥45° (preferably 60°) side slopes to reduce buildup; consider periodic drawdown or recycle piping to mobilize settled solids [C2‑1.2.5, p.262–263 in 2].

9) Force main and scour check
- Size and operate so minimum velocities prevent solids deposition while avoiding excessive head; on constant‑speed stations the selected pump/pump‑combination must achieve the minimum scour velocity in the force main [C2‑1.2.2, p.260–261 in 1; C2‑1.2.3, p.262 in 2].
- Consider cost‑benefit tradeoffs among force main diameter, pump selection, and surge protection [C2‑1.2.2, p.260–261 in 1].

10) Reliability and power
- Provide redundancy in pumps/motors/controls and provide standby power (portable for small stations or permanent with ATS for larger), sized to start/operate pumps at peak possible load plus ancillary equipment [C2‑1.8.2, p.266; C2‑1.8.3, p.266 in 3].

11) Instrumentation and verification
- Provide suitable flow‑measurement at the station and run timers on all pumps to verify actual flows and cycling against design assumptions [C2‑1.4, p.262 in 2].

12) Documentation and approvals
- Carry the above basis through the engineering report and plans/specifications sequence required by Ecology following the approved General Sewer Plan [G1‑2.2, p.33 in 9]. Include the pump curve with computed system curve, design data, O&M procedures, and contingency planning in the O&M manual [C2‑1.7, p.266 in 3].

Notes for the town of 400 (example summary, no growth assumed)
- ADF ≈ 24,000 gpd (16.7 gpm) using 60 gpcd [C1‑3.3.1, p.209 in 5; 5.3.1, p.99 in 8].
- Peak factor ≈ 3.7 (population 0.4k; ≥2.5 minimum) [C1‑3.3.2, p.209 in 5].
- PHF ≈ 88,800 gpd (≈62 gpm) [C1‑3.3.2, p.209 in 5].
- Pumps:
  - Duplex: 2 × 62 gpm each at design head (meets “largest out” criterion) [C2‑1.2.3, p.260 in 1].
  - Triplex: 3 × ~31 gpm each so two in service meet ~62 gpm (also meets criterion) [C2‑1.2.3, p.260 in 1; p.262 in 2].
- Wet well (constant‑speed, illustrative t = 10 min): Vactive ≈ 155 gal (duplex) or 77.5 gal (triplex) between on/off, plus added volumes for submergence, controls, and maintenance provisions; design geometry to minimize solids [C2‑1.2.5, p.262–263 in 2].
