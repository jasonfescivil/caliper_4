Master outline for a small-community (<1,000 population) wastewater treatment feasibility (engineering) report for Washington State. The outline follows WAC 173-240-060 “Engineering Reports,” Ecology’s Criteria for Sewage Works Design (Orange Book), and related federal references. Each section lists its purpose and citation anchors.

0. Cover page and Executive Summary
- Purpose: Identify the proponent, project intent, need, major alternatives, selected approach, costs, schedule, and key approvals at-a-glance.
- Anchors
  - WAC: Engineering report must be “sufficiently complete” for design without substantial changes (173-240-060(2)) [G1, p.48; 7].
  - Orange Book: Ecology’s review objective and approval sequence for planning/design/construction [G1-1 Objective, p.12; 4] and [G1-2.2 Sequence of Planning, p.33; 6].
  - Federal: NEPA/SERP framework when federal funds are involved [G1-2.8, SERP/NEPA, p.41; 10].

1. Introduction and Purpose of Report
- Purpose: Define the report as the project-specific engineering/feasibility report; state goals, scope, and how it fits the approved general sewer plan.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(2)–(3) report purpose and minimum required elements; consistency with approved general sewer plan [p.48; 7].
  - Orange Book: G1-2.2 planning sequence; G1-3.1 general sewer plan objectives and linkage to engineering reports [p.33; 6] and [p.41–43; 10].
  - Federal: SERP/NEPA applicability for CWSRF and other federal actions [p.41; 10].

2. Owner, Governance, and Contacts
- Purpose: Identify owner and authorized representative; describe who will own, operate, and maintain the system.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(3)(a) owner and representative [p.48; 7].
  - Orange Book: G1-3.2(2) “who will own, operate, and maintain the system” in general sewer plan content [p.41–43; 10].
  - Federal: N/A.

3. Project Description, Location, and Service Area
- Purpose: Describe the project, include maps of current/proposed service areas and location.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(3)(b) project description and maps [p.48; 7].
  - Orange Book: G1-3.2(3)-(4) service boundaries and required layout maps [p.41–43; 10]; If lacking an up-to-date general sewer plan, consult Ecology on which 173-240-050 elements to include [p.48; 7].
  - Federal: N/A.

4. Regulatory Framework and Permitting Path (State, Local, Tribal, Federal)
- Purpose: Summarize applicable regulations, permits, and review processes.
- Anchors
  - WAC/State:
    - Discharge standards/AKART (173-221; AKART expectations) [G3 intro, p.179; 2].
    - Water Quality Standards and mixing zones (173-201A; acute/chronic criteria; mixing zone; AKART prerequisite) [p.179; 2].
    - NPDES for surface water discharges (173-220; 5-year permits, limits, monitoring, public notice) [p.179; 2].
    - Ground Water Quality Standards (173-200; antidegradation, AKART, criteria) [p.179; 2].
    - State Waste Discharge permits (173-216; reclaimed water, exclusions) [p.179; 2].
    - Submission of plans/reports (173-240; engineering reports, plans/specs, O&M, certified operator, public ownership) [p.179; 2].
    - SEPA process and public participation (173-802; early checklist recommended) [p.179; 2].
  - Orange Book (local/tribal/other):
    - Local permits: building, shoreline, floodplain, air quality [G1-1.5.2–1.5.5, p.31–32; 8].
    - Tribal governments and consultation requirements [G1-1.6, p.31–32; 8].
  - Federal:
    - NEPA through SERP when federally funded (CWSRF) [G1-2.8/SERP, p.41; 10].

5. Population, Growth, and Planning Consistency
- Purpose: Document past and projected population/loadings consistent with adopted plans.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(3)(b) requires service area description; use OFM or other data with projections [p.48; 7].
  - Orange Book: G1-3.1 need for anticipated growth and timing in general sewer plans [p.41–43; 10].
  - Federal: N/A.

6. Existing Wastewater Flows and Loadings
- Purpose: Characterize current flows/loads, variability, and data quality; identify sources.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(3)(c) present/future quantity and quality including industrial [p.48–49; 7] (one full year of data; accredited lab; calibrated meters; diurnal/seasonal variations; domestic/commercial/industrial distribution; return streams).
  - Orange Book: G1 review objective (data supports compliance design) [p.12; 4].
  - Federal: N/A.

7. Infiltration/Inflow (I/I), Overflows, and Controls
- Purpose: Evaluate I/I impacts, SSOs/CSOs, and mitigation measures.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(j) I/I evaluation; at least one year testing; alternatives (rehab, EQ basins); SSO/CSO mapping and controls [p.53; 5].
  - Orange Book: Section C1-7 referenced for I/I investigations (as guidance pointer within Orange Book) [p.53; 5].
  - Federal: N/A.

8. Industrial Waste and Pretreatment
- Purpose: Identify industrial contributors and pretreatment needs to protect processes and biosolids.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(k) special provisions/pretreatment for significant industrial sources [p.53; 5].
  - Orange Book: Use treatability studies; ensure stable operation and protect receiving waters [p.53; 5].
  - Federal: N/A.

9. Receiving Water and Water Quality Compliance
- Purpose: Describe receiving water, classify standards, low-flow/tidal conditions, and demonstrate compliance at mixing zone boundary.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(3)(e) receiving water description; freshwater 7Q10; saltwater profiles; identify limiting criteria; WET testing (173-205 WAC) context; shellfish considerations [p.50; 3].
  - Orange Book: Ecology’s mixing zone evaluation requirements, model calibration expectations [p.50; 3].
  - Federal: EPA WQX/USGS/NOAA data sources acceptable; NSSP shellfish guidance reference [p.50; 3].

10. Outfall/Disposal Method and Mixing Zone Analysis
- Purpose: Present outfall location/design and demonstrate WQS compliance with acute/chronic mixing zones.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(l) outfall analysis; compliance with 173-201A-400 boundaries if allowed [p.53; 5].
  - Orange Book: Use Ecology’s mixing zone guidance; Visual PLUMES/CORMIX modeling; calibrate to field; cover critical flows/loadings [p.53; 5].
  - Federal: EPA “Technical Support Document for Water Quality-based Toxics Control” [p.53; 5].

11. Treatment Alternatives Analysis and Selected Process
- Purpose: Identify feasible alternatives (including no action), evaluate, and select preferred process meeting AKART and water quality needs.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(3)(f) alternatives evaluated; type of treatment based on wastewater character, disposal, degree required; reasons rejected; consider conservation and reclaimed water options [p.50–51; 3].
  - Orange Book:
    - G3 Small communities: technologies appropriate for small communities; supplemental criteria [G3 intro, p.175; 1].
    - AKART and when more than secondary may be needed [p.179; 2].
  - Federal: NPDES framework for technology- and WQ-based limits (via 173-220 program description) [p.179; 2].

12. Site Selection, Constraints, and Soils
- Purpose: Demonstrate site suitability, constraints, and geotechnical readiness for design.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060 supports site/design basis for plans/specs; engineering report completeness [p.48; 7].
  - Orange Book: Site assessments must consider tsunami inundation risk; historic/archaeological resources (consult DAHP/Tribal); and conduct adequate soils analyses at final alternates to avoid changed conditions [p.53; 5].
  - Federal: N/A.

13. Process Flow Diagram(s) and Hydraulic Profile(s)
- Purpose: Provide liquid/solids flow schematics, recycle/return streams, sampling/monitoring points, and detailed hydraulics for critical conditions.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240-060(i) flow diagram and hydraulic profile [p.53; 5].
  - Orange Book: Include monitoring locations that avoid recycle influence; site layout and topography; profiles for high/low plant and receiving water levels [p.53; 5].
  - Federal: N/A.

14. Operations, O&M Manuals, Certified Operator, and Ownership
- Purpose: Describe operational approach, O&M documentation, staffing/certification, and public ownership.
- Anchors
  - WAC: 173-240 requires O&M manual, certified operator, and public ownership in submittals [p.179; 2].
  - Orange Book: G1-4.4 Operation and Maintenance Manual framework (content and approval) listed in TOC [p.15; 9].
  - Federal: N/A.

15. Environmental Review and Public Participation
- Purpose: Complete SEPA; outline public engagement; identify NEPA-equivalent SERP steps if federally funded.
- Anchors
  - WAC/State: SEPA purpose and early checklist issuance; public information/opportunity [173-802; p.179; 2].
  - Orange Book/Federal: SERP elements that satisfy NEPA for CWSRF (interdisciplinary approach, documentation, public notice/participation, alternatives analysis) [p.41; 10].
  - Federal: As above (SERP/NEPA) [p.41; 10].

16. Financial Plan, User Rates, and Funding Strategy (Small Community Focus)
- Purpose: Identify financing sources, evaluate/set user rates and connection fees, and align with funding eligibility timelines.
- Anchors
  - Orange Book: G3-2 Identifying and Securing Resources; hiring a consultant; financial resources; user rates (flat/consumption/combination) and connection fees [p.175; 1].
  - State/Federal: Ecology funding eligibility and Combined Funding Program; contact Ecology early [G1-2.8, p.41–42; 10].
  - Federal: N/A.

17. Implementation Plan and Schedule
- Purpose: Provide permitting, design, funding, construction sequencing, and milestones through start-up.
- Anchors
  - WAC/Orange Book: Planning/design/construction sequence (general sewer plan per 173-240-050, then engineering report per 173-240-060, then plans & specs per 173-240-070) with Ecology approvals [G1-2.2, p.33; 6]. G1-3.1 requires timeline for bringing facilities online [p.41–43; 10].
  - Federal: NPDES issuance cycle (5-year; limits/monitoring/public notice) [p.179; 2].

18. Appendices
- Purpose: Provide data and supporting documentation for regulatory and design decisions.
- Typical contents and anchors
  - Receiving water data (EIM/WQX/USGS/NOAA) and 7Q10/salinity/temperature profiles [WAC 173-240-060(3)(e), p.50; 3].
  - Toxic pollutant scans/WET test plans (173-205 WAC reference) [p.50; 3].
  - Outfall modeling (Visual PLUMES/CORMIX) and EPA TSD references [p.53; 5].
  - I/I analyses and SSO/CSO maps [173-240-060(j), p.53; 5].
  - SEPA checklist and public comments [173-802, p.179; 2].
  - Cultural resources correspondence (DAHP/Tribal) and soils reports [p.53; 5].
  - General sewer plan excerpts establishing consistency [G1-3.2, p.41–43; 10].

Notes for small communities (<1,000 population)
- Use G3 Special Considerations for Small Community Systems to tailor technology selection, financing, and consultant selection to small systems [G3 intro and G3-2, p.175; 1].
- AKART is secondary treatment for domestic wastewater; additional treatment may be required to meet WQS [p.179; 2].
