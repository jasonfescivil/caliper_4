## Core Design Standards Documents

The primary design standards are contained in the **Criteria for Sewage Works Design (CSWD)** manual, also known as the "Orange Book" [4]. This manual establishes:
- Minimum requirements and limiting factors for review of sewage treatment works and sewerage system plans [5]
- Design standards for ensuring consistency with public health, water quality, and biosolids management objectives [5]
- Requirements that lead to approvable plans and specifications, which state regulation WAC 173-240-040 requires reasonable consistency with [5]

## Regulatory Framework

The design standards follow a three-tier approval process [7]:

1. **General Sewer Plan** (WAC 173-240-050) [7]
   - Comprehensive plan for sewer systems
   - Must include preliminary engineering information demonstrating technical and financial feasibility [3]
   - Required content specified in G1-3.2 [3]

2. **Engineering Report** (WAC 173-240-060) [8]
   - Detailed, project and site-specific evaluations of alternatives
   - Must contain appropriate preliminary design detail
   - Must establish rationale for specific design criteria

3. **Plans and Specifications** (WAC 173-240-070) [8]
   - Detailed construction documents
   - Must be submitted after engineering report approval

## Environmental Review Standards

All projects must comply with environmental review standards [6]:
- **SEPA** (State Environmental Policy Act) - Required for all local agency actions [6]
- **NEPA** (National Environmental Policy Act) - Required for federally funded projects [3]
- **SERP** (State Environmental Review Process) - EPA-approved process for CWSRF-funded projects [3]
