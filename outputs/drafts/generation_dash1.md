Based on the provided documents, here are the governing requirements and definitions for Washington State wastewater facility planning and design relevant to an Engineering Report.

### Required Contents of an Engineering Report

An engineering report is a comprehensive document that examines the engineering and administrative aspects of a wastewater facility project [15, p.37; WAC 173-240-020(6)]. It must be sufficiently complete for the development of plans and specifications without substantial changes [8, p.6; WAC 173-240-060(2)]. A "substantial change" is defined as a change in treatment process, facility size, design criteria, or environmental impacts [14, p.48].

Per WAC 173-240-060, the report must include the following, among other items:
*   **Project Description**: A location map and a map of the present and proposed service area [8, p.6; WAC 173-240-060(3)(b)]. This includes population data for the service area for at least the last two decades and projections for the design period [14, p.48].
*   **Wastewater Quantity and Quality**: A statement on the present and expected future quantity and quality of wastewater, including industrial wastes [8, p.6; WAC 173-240-060(3)(c)]. The analysis must characterize waste loading (flow, BOD5, TSS), identify sources, and assess diurnal and seasonal variations based on at least one full year of data from an accredited lab [14, p.48].
*   **Treatment and Design**: The degree of treatment required, a description of the proposed process with an evaluation of alternatives, and the basic design data and sizing calculations for each unit [8, p.6; WAC 173-240-060(3)(d, f, g)].
*   **Infiltration and Inflow (I/I)**: A discussion of I/I problems, overflows, bypasses, and proposed corrections [7, p.7; WAC 173-240-060(3)(j)].
*   **Siting and Outfall**: A discussion of available sites, a flow diagram, a hydraulic profile, and a detailed outfall analysis [7, p.7; WAC 173-240-060(3)(h, i, l)].
*   **Cost Analysis**: An estimate of capital, operation, and maintenance costs for the life of the project, presented in total annual cost and present worth [7, p.7; WAC 173-240-060(3)(p)].
*   **Compliance**: Statements regarding compliance with applicable water quality management plans, the State Environmental Policy Act (SEPA), and the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) [7, p.7; WAC 173-240-060(3)(q, r)].

### Design Flows

The provided context does not contain specific definitions or recommended percentile choices for design flows such as Average Dry Weather Flow (ADWF), Peak Dry Weather Flow (PDWF), or peaking factors. However, it does require the engineering report to include a detailed analysis of wastewater flows, including:
*   Characterization of waste loading, including flow [14, p.48].
*   Assessment of diurnal and seasonal flow and loading variations [14, p.48].
*   Current infiltration and inflow (I/I) flows [14, p.48].
*   A discussion of I/I problems, overflows, and bypasses [7, p.7; WAC 173-240-060(3)(j)].
*   Consideration of peak wet-weather flows (WWF) and normal dry-weather flows for capacity assurance planning [6, p.12].

### Ecology/WA Guidance and Design Criteria

The term "AKART" (All Known, Available, and Reasonable methods of prevention, control, and Treatment) does not appear in the provided documents. However, the regulations establish the design criteria and guidance that Ecology uses for review, which serves a similar function.
*   **WAC 173-240-040 (Review Standards)**: The Department of Ecology reviews engineering reports and plans to ensure proposed facilities will be designed and operated to meet effluent limitations and the requirements of chapters 90.48 and 90.54 RCW for preventing and controlling water pollution [3, p.4].
*   **Criteria for Sewage Works Design (CSWD)**: State regulation requires that submitted documents be "reasonably consistent" with the state of Washington's "Criteria for sewage works design" (also known as the Orange Book) [3, p.4; WAC 173-240-040(2)]. This manual establishes the minimum requirements and limiting factors used by Ecology for the review of wastewater facility plans [4, p.5].

### Cross-References to WAC 173-240

The planning and design process follows a sequence outlined in the regulations:
*   **WAC 173-240-050 (General Sewer Plan)**: This comprehensive plan for a sewer system is a prerequisite for detailed project planning [5, p.2; General sewer plan]. An engineering report must incorporate the relevant items from this plan unless an up-to-date version is already on file with Ecology [8, p.6; WAC 173-240-060(1)].
*   **WAC 173-240-060 (Engineering Report)**: This is the core project-planning document that provides a detailed, site-specific evaluation of alternatives to implement projects described in the general sewer plan [8, p.6; 15, p.37].
*   **WAC 173-240-070 (Plans and Specifications)**: These are the detailed construction documents developed from an approved engineering report [7, p.7; WAC 173-240-070(1)]. Their content and format must conform to the "Criteria for sewage works design" [7, p.7].
