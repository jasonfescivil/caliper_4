Based on the provided context, the design standards for sewage works include several key regulatory frameworks and technical requirements:

## Primary Design Standards Framework

**WAC 173-240 Series Regulations:**
- **WAC 173-240-050**: Requirements for General Sewer Plans [3,7]
- **WAC 173-240-060**: Requirements for Engineering Reports [8] 
- **WAC 173-240-070**: Requirements for Plans and Specifications [8]

## Planning and Design Sequence

The design standards follow a three-phase approach [7]:

1. **General Sewer Plan** (WAC 173-240-050): Must include preliminary engineering information demonstrating technical and financial feasibility, service boundaries, layout maps, and facility descriptions [3]

2. **Engineering Report** (WAC 173-240-060): Detailed project-specific evaluations of alternatives with preliminary design detail sufficient for any engineer to develop complete design documents [8]

3. **Plans and Specifications** (WAC 173-240-070): Detailed construction drawings and specifications for facility construction [8]

## Environmental Review Standards

**Required Environmental Compliance:**
- **SEPA** (State Environmental Policy Act): Required for all local agency actions including general sewer plans and engineering reports [6]
- **NEPA** (National Environmental Policy Act): Required for projects receiving federal funding or approvals [3,6]
- **SERP** (State Environmental Review Process): EPA-approved "NEPA-like" process for CWSRF-funded projects [3,6]

## Technical Design References

**Industry Standards:**
- **Ten-State Standards**: Widely referenced for sewer system design, providing minimum design criteria for flow conveyance and peak design flow estimation [2]
- **Professional Engineering Requirements**: All documents must be prepared under supervision of a Washington-licensed professional engineer [3,8]

The standards ensure facilities meet "effluent limitations and other wastewater discharge permit terms and conditions necessary to protect public health and the environment" per RCW Chapters 90.48, 90.52, and 90.54 [1,4].
