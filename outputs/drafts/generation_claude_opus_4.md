## Core Design Standards Documents

The primary design standards are established in the **Criteria for Sewage Works Design (CSWD)** manual, also known as the "Orange Book" [4]. The manual's goals include establishing "a basis for the design and review of plans and specifications for sewage treatment works and sewerage systems" and "minimum requirements and limiting factors utilized by the Washington Department of Ecology" [5].

## Regulatory Framework

The design standards are implemented through Washington Administrative Code (WAC) 173-240, which requires:

- **General Sewer Plans** must comply with WAC 173-240-050 [7]
- **Engineering Reports** must comply with WAC 173-240-060 [8]
- **Plans and Specifications** must comply with WAC 173-240-070 [8]

State regulation WAC 173-240-040 requires "reasonable consistency with the CSWD requirements" [5].

## Environmental Review Requirements

All projects must undergo environmental reviews:
- **SEPA** (State Environmental Policy Act) - required for all local agency actions [6]
- **NEPA** (National Environmental Policy Act) - required for federally funded projects [3]
- **SERP** (State Environmental Review Process) - EPA-approved process for CWSRF-funded projects [3]

## Technical Standards References

The design standards incorporate:
- The "Ten-State Standards" for sewer system design criteria [2]
- American Public Health Association (APHA), ASCE, AWWA, and WEF glossary for terminology [1]
- Various professional organization standards (ANSI, ASTM, IEEE, NFPA, etc.) [1]

## Design Review Purpose

Ecology reviews these documents "to ascertain that the proposed facilities will be designed, constructed, operated, and maintained to meet the effluent limitations and other wastewater discharge permit terms and conditions necessary to protect public health and the environment" [4].
