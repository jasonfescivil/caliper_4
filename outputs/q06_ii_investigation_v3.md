assistant: I/I Plan for Small Washington Systems

1) Purpose and regulatory framework
- Purpose: Locate, quantify, and prioritize infiltration/inflow (I/I) sources; correlate wet-weather responses; and develop corrective actions consistent with Washington State standards and EPA guidance.
- Applicable standards and guidance:
  - WA Criteria for Sewage Works Design (“Orange Book”)—projects must be reasonably consistent with CSWD requirements; Ecology reviews plans/reports under WAC 173-240 [22; 23; 17 (WAC 173‑240‑040, ‑050)].
  - SSES Phase I tasks per EPA: interviews, map study, flow diagrams, dry vs. wet-weather flow, preliminary field survey/selective flow tests, determination of excessive/non-excessive I/I, and a plan/budget/timetable [13, PHASE I].
  - WEF FD-6 (Sanitary Sewer Rehabilitation): technology applications and field methods for I/I including smoke testing, CCTV, dyed-water flooding, flow monitoring, and night flow isolation [2 (4.4); 7 (5.2.x); 8 (4.4.6); 1 (4.4.8); 15 (2.x)].
  - NPDES permits may require I/I evaluations and reporting (e.g., use monitoring records; submit report; corrective plan if I/I increased >15%) [10, 1 Introduction].
  - Monitoring and reporting conditions framework (EPA Permit Writers’ Manual, Ch. 8) [28].

2) SSES approach and phases
- Phase I (EPA):
  - a) Patterned interviews
  - b) Sanitary and storm sewer map study
  - c) System flow diagrams
  - d) Dry vs wet weather flow determinations
  - e) Preliminary field survey and selective flow tests
  - f) Determination of excessive or non-excessive I/I
  - g) Plan of action, budget, timetable [13, PHASE I].
- Technology applications to deploy (WEF):
  - Manhole/cleanout inspections; pipeline lists and manhole lists; smoke testing; CCTV; dyed-water flooding/quantification; wastewater flow monitoring; night flow isolation [2, 4.4.1–4.4.8].

3) Monitoring program (periods, scope, locations)
- Scope: Plan a meaningful flow-monitoring project based on master planning/I&I/SSO/CMOM objectives; define data accuracy needs and project duration/size accordingly [15, 2.1, p.126].
- Use historical data: Incorporate WRRF records, pump station on–off records, rainfall, long-term flow-monitoring where accurate [15, 2.2, p.126].
- Periods to capture:
  - Dry- and wet-weather periods and diurnal/weekly/seasonal variation—“ideally” data show flows for dry/wet weather and at different times of day, week, and season [15, p.126].
  - Minimum-flow periods (night flow isolation) to assess groundwater infiltration (GWI) [1, 4.4.8, p.78].
- Monitoring scope and siting: Identify key basins by tracing upstream to downstream and flagging problem areas (flooding, SSOs, blockages, etc.) to set meter locations [15, 2.3 and map-tracing steps, p.126–127].
- Rainfall correlation: Quantify rainfall-derived I/I (RDII). Use rainfall records and accepted methods (e.g., SWMM with RTK/SUH approaches) to relate rainfall to sewer response [3, List of Acronyms (RDII, SWMM, RTK, SUH, UH)].

4) Field investigations
- Smoke testing (WEF FD-6, Sec. 5.2):
  - Setup: Blower forces non-toxic, odorless, non-staining smoke; use sandbags/inflatable plugs to isolate shorter sections and increase findings [7, 5.2.4–5.2.7].
  - Plugs: Pneumatic plugs; compressed air; taglines; insertion/removal safety cautions (high forces even at low heads) [7, 5.2.5].
  - Testing methodology and documentation: Ensure smoke throughout test section; check nearby storm system; walk test area; photo/video all leaks; map and uniquely identify each leak; QA/QC notes provided [7, 5.2.6–5.2.7].
- Dyed-water flooding and leak quantification:
  - Purpose: Pinpoint inflow/RDII sources and quantify selected leaks using field measurements and empirical values; use portable weirs, hydrostatic devices, or container-time tests; coordinate with CCTV [8, 4.4.6, p.77].
  - Public notification: Notify utilities/regulators in advance (dye may be seen in water bodies) [5, 5.3.1, p.212].
  - Storm sewer testing procedure: Plug downstream storm segments; add water+dye to ≤ half pipe diameter; monitor downstream sanitary manhole for dye; caution to prevent overfilling/flooding; safety for plug use applies [5, 5.3.2, p.212].
- Night flow isolation:
  - Divide major basins into smaller areas; take low-flow readings during minimum-flow periods to identify excessive GWI needing further investigation [1, 4.4.8, p.78].
- CCTV:
  - Use internal inspection in conjunction with dyed-water results to document sources and support rehab selection [5, p.212; 8, 4.4.6].

5) QA/QC
- Smoke testing QA/QC: Use plugs/sandbags to improve results; prepare smoke sketch (setup, crew, date); verify smoke coverage; document storm cross-transfer; walk entire area; photo/video and map all leaks with identifiers [7, 5.2.7].
- Dyed-water: Use biodegradable fluorescent dye; maintain test records/forms; control test heads/volumes to avoid impacts; monitor sanitary manholes for dye appearance [5, 5.3, p.212].
- Flow monitoring QA: Define required data accuracy for the project; incorporate vetted historical datasets; ensure data cover dry/wet periods and minimum-flow windows [15, 2.1–2.2, p.126].
- Safety: Follow confined space and plug-handling cautions (Orange Book safety chapter outlines safety regulations and confined spaces) [14, G2‑7].
- Recordkeeping and O&M: Include sampling/monitoring and recordkeeping procedures in the O&M manual per WAC 173-240-080 [27, Operation and Maintenance Manual Requirements].
- Construction QA plan (if installing permanent metering facilities): Provide inspection/testing, documentation, and change control per WAC 173-240-075 [27].

6) Data analysis and rainfall correlation
- Separate flows into BWF/DWF, GWI, and RDII using flow data, pump on–off records, and rainfall [3, Acronyms (BWF, DWF, GWI, RDII)].
- Use recognized modeling tools/methods (e.g., SWMM, RTK/SUH) to relate hyetographs to sewer hydrographs and predict RDII [3].
- Use WEF guidance to interpret system behavior and support capacity planning to avoid over/under-sizing [15, p.126–127].

7) Deliverables
- SSES Phase I report (EPA): Includes items (a)–(g): interviews; maps; flow diagrams; DWF vs WWF; selective tests; determination of excessive I/I; plan, budget, timetable [13].
- Maps and plans per WAC 173-240 general sewer plan standards: service boundaries; existing/proposed sewers and pump stations; flow directions; topography; water bodies [17, WAC 173‑240‑050(3)(d)].
- Monitoring plan and report: Monitoring locations, periods, data accuracy statement; raw and processed flow/rainfall data; DWF/BWF/GWI/RDII separation; findings from smoke/dye/CCTV; prioritized rehab recommendations (per WEF technology results) [15 (monitoring planning), 2; 5, 8].
- If required by NPDES permit: I/I evaluation report using monitoring records; summary of measurable I/I; if >15% increase from prior (on equivalent rainfall), include plan/schedule to locate and correct sources [10, 1 Introduction].
- O&M manual updates: Monitoring, sampling, and recordkeeping procedures per WAC 173-240-080(4) [27].
- Monitoring and reporting conditions consistent with EPA Permit Writers’ Manual Ch. 8 (locations, frequency, sample collection, reporting, recordkeeping) [28].

Field checklist (for crews)
- Pre-field
  - Confirm permits/notifications (dyed-water testing to utilities/regulators) [5, 5.3.1].
  - Safety plan (confined space, plug safety) [14, G2‑7; 7, 5.2.5].
  - Assemble equipment: smoke blower; non-toxic smoke candles (3–5 min burn) [7, 5.2.4]; inflatable plugs with air supply and taglines [7, 5.2.5]; fluorescent dye and hoses/hydrant access [5, 5.3.2]; portable weirs/hydrostatic devices [8].
- Flow monitoring
  - Install at key sites identified in monitoring scope [15, 2.3].
  - Verify data logging; note rainfall/pump on–off data sources [15, 2.2].
- Night flow isolation
  - Subdivide basins; schedule minimum-flow period measurements; record low-flow readings [1, 4.4.8].
- Smoke testing
  - Set plugs/sandbags to isolate sections [7, 5.2.4–5.2.5].
  - Operate blower; verify smoke reaches entire test section [7, 5.2.6].
  - Check nearby storm system for smoke transfer [7, 5.2.7].
  - Walk area; document/photo/video all leaks; map with unique IDs [7, 5.2.7].
- Dyed-water testing
  - Plug downstream storm segment(s); add water+dye to ≤ half pipe diameter; monitor sanitary manhole for dye [5, 5.3.2].
  - Avoid overfilling; monitor downstream levels; document times/volumes [5, 5.3.2].
  - Coordinate CCTV where dye is observed [5; 8].
- Post-field
  - QA/QC check of sketches, photos, forms; compile data for analysis [7, 5.2.7; 5, fig./forms reference].

Minimum instrumentation and materials
- Flow measurement
  - Temporary open-channel flow monitoring suitable for sewers with recording capability; place at key locations; leverage established methods (see Isco Open Channel Flow Measurement Handbook reference cited by Ecology) [4, G2‑10 References; 15, 2.1–2.3].
  - Pump station on–off recording (existing or added) [15, 2.2].
  - Portable weirs/hydrostatic devices for localized leak quantification [8, 4.4.6].
- Rainfall
  - Local rainfall records/gauges adequate to develop hyetographs for RDII analysis (for SWMM/RTK/SUH methods) [15, 2.2; 3 (SWMM, RTK, SUH acronyms)].
- Inspection and testing
  - Smoke testing: blower; non-toxic, odorless, non-staining smoke candles (3–5 min); sandbags/inflatable plugs; documentation tools [7, 5.2.4–5.2.7].
  - Dyed-water testing: biodegradable fluorescent dye; hoses/hydrant or water trucks; inflatable plugs/sandbags; dye testing forms; CCTV [5, 5.3; 8, 4.4.6].
  - CCTV equipment per WEF technology applications list [2, 4.4.5].
- Isolation and safety
  - Inflatable plugs (pneumatic; with compressed air, winch as needed; taglines; deflation from ground level) and strict adherence to safety cautions [7, 5.2.5].
- Data management and reporting
  - Procedures to store, QA/QC, and report monitoring data per O&M/monitoring guidance (WAC 173-240-080; EPA Permit Writers’ Manual Ch. 8) [27; 28].

Notes on compliance and submittals in WA
- Ecology review for consistency with CSWD and WAC 173-240 (plans, engineering reports, O&M) [17 (WAC 173‑240‑040, ‑050); 24 (abbrev./defs)].
- Plans for sewers must include plan/profile, boundaries, flows, pump stations, topography, water bodies, utilities [35, G1‑4.2.2(B)].
- For small communities, see CSWD G3 chapter as supplemental planning guidance [4, G3].

Acronyms used: BWF, DWF, GWI, RDII, SWMM, RTK, SUH, WWTP, SSO, CMOM (see EPA list) [3].
