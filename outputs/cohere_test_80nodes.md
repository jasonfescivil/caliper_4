In Washington State, **engineering reports** under Chapter G1 must comply with the following requirements [4,9,16,18,19,21]:

1. **Definition & Purpose**:  
   A comprehensive analysis documenting engineering alternatives and environmental impacts for a project, serving as the basis for design [4,9]. It must be sufficiently detailed to allow another engineer to develop plans/specifications **without substantial changes** (defined as alterations to process, size, design criteria, performance standards, environmental impacts, or total project cost) [19].

2. **Preparation & Licensing**:  
   Prepared under the supervision of a **Washington-licensed professional engineer** and include their seal/signature [4,9,21]. For federal funding (e.g., USDA-RD, EPA), follow the interagency Preliminary Engineering Report (PER) template [9].

3. **Required Content**:  
   Include [18,21]:  
   - Project description, owner/contact info, and service area maps.  
   - Existing/future wastewater quantity/quality data (flow, BOD₅, TSS, industrial wastes).  
   - Treatment process justification, design calculations, and expected effluent quality.  
   - Site evaluation (including 25/100-year floodplain analysis).  
   - Compliance with SEPA/NEPA (if applicable) and water quality standards.  
   - Cost estimates (capital/operations/maintenance) and funding method.  

4. **Exceptions**:  
   Reports are **not required** for sewer line extensions or pump stations that conform to an approved General Sewer Plan *unless* they involve overflows, bypasses, or discharges to overloaded systems [16].

5. **Approval Process**:  
   Submit three copies to Ecology for approval *before construction*. Must align with an approved General Sewer Plan (WAC 173-240-050) unless waived [18].

These requirements ensure technical feasibility, regulatory compliance, and financial sustainability for wastewater projects.
