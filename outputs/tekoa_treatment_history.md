assistant: 1) Narrative (2000–present)

Based on the most recent engineering documentation, as of August 2025 the Tekoa WWTP process train consists of headworks with a grit chamber and a self-cleaning screen, receiving influent from the lift station and scum overflow from the secondary clarifier; flow then proceeds to a cleanout (with a RAS connection) and into an HDPE‑lined earthen aeration basin with aeration in the basin center, followed by a single secondary clarifier, UV disinfection (redundant bulb banks in a U‑shaped channel), and effluent flow metering prior to discharge [complete-report-08-22-2025.docx, p.64, “7. Effluent Discharge”]. The secondary clarifier scum is recycled to headworks; thickened solids are returned as RAS or wasted (WAS) to an abandoned lagoon; the aerobic digester is currently non‑operational [complete-report-08-22-2025.docx, p.64].

Legacy design/operations referenced in the O&M materials (last updated 2023) indicate prior chlorine contact and dechlorination systems, with sulfur dioxide (SO2) feed guidance and corrosion cautions for gas systems; the addendum notes that operators should monitor SO2 to ensure dechlorination compliance [2023_tekoa_stp_om_manual_addendum.pdf, p.43]. Table III in the O&M addendum lists Hydroscreen 780 screening (0.01‑inch opening), a bar screen, two air blowers for aeration, one clarifier, and gas chlorination/dechlorination units; it also lists a design population of 1,000. Average/peak flow capacities (ADF/PF, MGD) are not reliably stated in the excerpted table and are therefore unknown here [2023_tekoa_stp_om_manual_addendum.pdf, p.36, “Table III – Treatment System Design Parameters”].

Between 2000 and the present, the provided documents substantiate that disinfection is UV by 2025, supplanting the legacy chlorination/dechlorination system; the conversion date is not given in the excerpts [complete-report-08-22-2025.docx, p.64; 2023_tekoa_stp_om_manual_addendum.pdf, p.36, p.43]. Aeration/digestion volumes in the O&M record were reconciled, with a stated agreement that the aeration basin is 47,000 gal and the digester 36,500 gal; however, no construction change is identified in the record provided [2023_tekoa_stp_om_manual_addendum.pdf, p.43]. No flow equalization, filtration, SCADA, or outfall location changes are documented in the excerpts (unknown).
