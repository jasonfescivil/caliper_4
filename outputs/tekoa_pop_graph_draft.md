Narrative on approach, conservatism, and WAC compliance
Using the OFM Whitman County medium projection series as the driver and applying those growth rates to Tekoa’s OFM base-year city estimate is a conservative, standards-based approach for facility planning. It aligns the city’s projections to the officially adopted countywide medium scenario and avoids overfitting to short-term local variability. This approach is conservative for a small city because county averages typically smooth localized spikes, producing tempered growth assumptions for design sizing. It also reduces the risk of over-sizing infrastructure in response to transient in-migration or single-project surges. The Tekoa facility plan states the planning horizons are five years and twenty years (to 2030 and 2045) for wastewater capacity planning [6] (tekoa-facility-plan-draft-v41.docx; §3.8), satisfying WAC 173-240-060(3)(b)’s expectation for 20-year planning. Furthermore, the plan indicates OFM countywide projections were applied directly to Tekoa’s population [6] (§3.8), which helps maintain consistency with regional planning assumptions and supports coordination with countywide planning policies. Once the primary OFM medium-series growth rates and the Tekoa base-year city estimate are provided, the projection is implemented mechanically by compounding the annual county medium growth rates from the base year to each target year. Converting those projected populations to ERUs must follow Tekoa’s adopted method (e.g., persons per ERU based on OFM household size, or flow per ERU if Tekoa defines ERU hydraulically); using the local ERU standard ensures consistency with the City’s historical permitting, billing, and design basis. Key limitations are: county-average growth may understate or overstate Tekoa’s trajectory if local drivers diverge; the ERU conversion introduces uncertainty if persons/household or gpd/ERU are outdated; and any GMA allocation decisions (if available) could refine the share to Tekoa. Despite these, the OFM-medium approach provides a transparent, reproducible, and regulator-familiar basis for sizing facilities to average annual day flow and related design criteria over the 20-year horizon, with clear traceability to state-vetted demographic inputs.
