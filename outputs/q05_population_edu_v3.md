assistant: Below is a compliant approach you can use to develop population and EDU projections using OFM sources for a small Washington community. All requirements and methods are taken from WAC 173-240-060 and Ecology’s Orange Book guidance as provided.

Service area delineation (WAC 173-240-060(3)(b))
- Prepare a project description with:
  - A location map and a map of the current and proposed sewer service area, scaled so at least one map shows the complete current and proposed service areas and how they relate to adjacent service areas [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].
  - A map showing existing collection system changes and proposed locations of land application of wastewater [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].
- The community must certify that its general sewer plan adequately addresses current conditions and the service area, or consult Ecology on what portions of Section 050 to include in the engineering report if it does not have an adequate, up-to-date plan [1, p.48].

Data sources and baseline (use OFM)
- Provide OFM or other population data for the service area for at least the last two decades, if available [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].
- Include population projections for the project planning area and concentrated growth areas for the project design period [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].
- Base projections on historical records cited in the approved general sewer plan and ensure consistency with data from appropriate local land use planning agencies [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].

Planning horizon
- Estimate future conditions using a planning horizon that is normally 20 years from the date of the report (align population/EDU projections with this horizon to support future waste load estimates) [1, p.48, 060(3)(c), Explanation].

Growth rates and projections (method)
- Derive growth rates from the OFM time series for at least the last two decades for the defined service area and subareas (including concentrated growth areas), ensuring consistency with the approved general sewer plan and local land use planning agency data [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].
- Document the chosen projection approach and how it ties to the approved general sewer plan’s historical records and local planning data (to ensure an engineer can develop plans without substantial changes) [1, p.48, 060(2); 060(3)(b), Explanation].

Land use and zoning considerations
- Keep projections consistent with appropriate local land use planning agency data and identify concentrated growth areas on your maps and in your allocation methodology [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].

EDU projections
- Unknown: The provided context does not define EDUs or a conversion method. Use locally adopted assumptions/methods from the approved general sewer plan or local planning standards, and document them in the engineering report so plans and specifications can be developed without substantial changes [1, p.48, 060(2); 060(3)(b), Explanation].

Uncertainty
- Unknown: The provided context does not prescribe an uncertainty method for population/EDU projections. Ensure the report is “sufficiently complete” and note that any substantial change (e.g., to facility size, design criteria, or environmental impacts) requires an amendment to the approved engineering report [1, p.48, 060(2)].
- Maintain consistency with OFM and local planning data; if new data materially change growth assumptions, update projections and pursue amendments as needed per “substantial change” criteria [1, p.48, 060(2)].

Process checklist (with citations)
1) Confirm general sewer plan adequacy or consult Ecology on required Section 050 elements [1, p.48].  
2) Delineate the service area and prepare required maps (current/proposed areas, relation to adjacent areas, existing system changes, and proposed land application locations) [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].  
3) Compile OFM population data for at least the last two decades for the service area and subareas [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].  
4) Set a planning horizon of normally 20 years from the date of the report [1, p.48, 060(3)(c), Explanation].  
5) Project population for the planning area and concentrated growth areas based on historical records in the approved general sewer plan and consistent with local land use planning agency data [1, p.48, 060(3)(b), Explanation].  
6) Convert population projections to EDUs using locally adopted methods from the general sewer plan (method not provided in the context) and document assumptions for Ecology [1, p.48, 060(2); 060(3)(b), Explanation].  
7) Address uncertainty by documenting data sources and assumptions; monitor for substantial changes that would necessitate an amendment [1, p.48, 060(2)].

Notes
- Ecology does not normally require an engineering report for sewer line extensions or pump stations that conform to an Ecology-approved general sewer plan [1, p.48].
