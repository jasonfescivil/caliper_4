Outline – Wastewater Facilities Feasibility Report (Washington)

- Cover, Executive Summary, and Report Purpose
  - Purpose and need; planning objectives; 20-year planning horizon for public health and environmental protection [1], [5]
  - Owner/operator and O&M responsibility; authorized representatives [1], [11]

- Scope and Study Area
  - Service boundaries; vicinity; present and proposed service area maps [1], [11]
  - Planning/coordination approach and submittals (Ecology engineering approval framework; PER best-practice template) [4]

- Existing Conditions
  - System overview: treatment, conveyance, pump stations, monitoring/control, discharges/overflows; capacity and compliance status; known/foreseeable problems; human health and water quality considerations [1]
  - Mapping: sewers (location/size/slope/flow/capacity), pump stations/force mains, topography/elevations/streets, surface waters and discharge points [1], [11]
  - Population trends and projections; method and agency concurrence [11]
  - Industrial contributors and wastewater characteristics (present/future) [11]
  - Infiltration and inflow; overflows/bypasses; existing I/I data and controls [9]
  - Receiving waters: description, water quality standards, how standards will be met outside/at mixing zones; key flow/hydraulic and oceanographic parameters [13]
  - Nearby wastewater facilities (within 20 miles and same drainage basin) [11]
  - Water systems and wells near facilities/outfalls [11]

- Needs Assessment
  - Regulatory drivers, growth, and foreseeable water quality issues; alignment with land use and comprehensive plans [1]
  - Treatment and disposal needs; degree of treatment required [11]
  - Reclaimed water opportunities (if applicable) and feasibility linkages [3], [5], [10]

- Alternatives Development
  - Required range: no action; conventional and advanced treatment; lagoons/land treatment; regionalization; reclaimed water; nonstructural/operational changes [3], [13]
  - Siting options: topography; flood risk (25-yr/100-yr), coastal SLR/tsunami; wetlands; soils; zoning; historic/archeological/cultural resources; proximity to residences; environmental justice; community impacts [3], [9], [14]
  - Disposal/outfall options: surface water outfalls/mixing zones; groundwater/land application options (include hydrogeologic factors if applicable) [8], [14]
  - Residuals/biosolids management options (beneficial use vs. disposal) [8]

- Evaluation Criteria and Methods
  - Environmental impacts; present-worth life-cycle cost; technical complexity and certified operator availability; project risk; legal/regulatory barriers; community/social considerations (including EJ); conservation of water/energy/waste [3], [13]
  - Compliance with water quality standards, WET considerations; mixing zone analysis methods (e.g., Visual PLUMES/CORMIX; EPA TSD) [8], [13]
  - I/I impacts and mitigation approaches in treatment sizing/performance [9]

- Alternatives Analysis Results
  - Screening outcomes and reasons for rejection [3]
  - Preliminary design data for final candidates; unit process selection, sizing basis, and expected performance (to support cost and feasibility ranking) [3]
  - Comparative costs: capital, O&M, total annual cost, present worth; method of assessment and accuracy [8]
  - Staffing and testing requirements per alternative; operator classification needs [8]
  - Outfall/mixing zone results; receiving water compliance demonstration [8]
  - Land application geohydrology (if applicable): soils, groundwater depth/movement, water balance, cumulative effects; sewer availability; reserve areas [14]

- Preferred Alternative
  - Selection rationale; conformance with evaluation criteria; provisions for future needs/expandability [8]
  - If not lowest life-cycle cost, document rationale and community support [3]

- Basis of Design (Preliminary)
  - Design flows/loads and effluent requirements; degree of treatment [11]
  - Process description and preliminary sizing calculations for each unit; expected efficiencies and overall performance [3], [11], [13]
  - Flow diagram(s), recycle/return flows, sampling/monitoring locations; hydraulic profiles for critical conditions; site layout with topography [9], [14]
  - Outfall concept and mixing zone compliance summary [8]
  - Residuals/biosolids management plan outline [8]

- Implementation Plan
  - Phasing and timeline to bring facilities online; adaptive approach to uncertainties [1]
  - Project development and review: early coordination with Ecology; expected submittals; review timelines (≥60 days); electronic document requirements [4]
  - Funding strategy and eligibility; early contact with Ecology’s Combined Funding Program; use of interagency PER template (USDA-RD/EPA/HUD/IHS) as best practice [1], [4]

- Permitting and Environmental Review
  - SEPA compliance statement; NEPA applicability (federal funding/approvals); SERP pathway for CWSRF, leveraging SEPA with expanded public participation [1], [11], [14]
  - Public notice and participation commitments to meet SERP/NEPA-like standards [1]
  - Water quality compliance framework (WAC 173-201A; mixing zones), and related permit considerations (e.g., WET) [13]

- Public Involvement Plan
  - Engagement approach: workshops/meetings/surveys; documentation of input [3]
  - Topics: project need; feasible technologies; environmental and community impacts; environmental justice; rate impacts [3]
  - SERP public notice/participation milestones [1]

- Operations, Staffing, and Testing
  - Facility classification; certified operator requirements; supporting staff roles; justification of adequate staffing [8]

- Cost and Financing
  - Opinion of probable cost: capital and O&M; total annual cost and present worth; method of assessing costs/expenses [8]
  - Rate and affordability considerations (for engagement) [3]

- Compliance Statements
  - Consistency with Ecology design standards and accepted engineering practice [2]
  - Consistency with applicable water quality management plans [11], [14]
  - SEPA/NEPA/SERP status [1], [11], [14]

- References and Standards
  - Ecology Criteria for Sewage Works Design, Chapter G1 (General Engineering Requirements) and WAC 173-240 (Engineering Report/Plans) [2], [4], [6], [11], [13], [14]
  - EPA: Technical Support Document for Water Quality-based Toxics Control; mixing zone analysis guidance; interagency PER template recognition [4], [8]
  - WEF/APHA/ASCE references (e.g., WEF MOP 11; APHA/ASCE/AWWA glossary) for terminology and practices [6], [5]

Notes:
- Where reclaimed water is part of the project, integrate Ecology’s reclaimed water feasibility and engineering report elements and coordinate with DOH as applicable; consider a single report where practical [5], [10].
