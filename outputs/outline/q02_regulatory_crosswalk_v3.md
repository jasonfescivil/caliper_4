assistant: Key points
- Planning: Ecology must approve general sewer plans, engineering reports, and plans/specs before construction; content and sequence are set in WAC 173-240 and the Orange Book (CSWD) [13][24][35][34]. SEPA (and SERP/NEPA when applicable) must be addressed during planning and prior to Ecology approvals [36][14].
- Design: Designs must be reasonably consistent with CSWD; Ecology also recognizes WEF, ASCE, APWA, and EPA design references for review [15][22]. For reclaimed water, follow WAC 173-219 (Purple Book) and coordinate with Ecology/Health [20][29][33].
- Permitting: 
  - NPDES for surface water discharges (Ecology as delegated authority) [20][2].
  - State Waste Discharge (SWD) for discharges to ground and certain discharges to sewers; can be combined with NPDES in one permit [20][31][7].
  - Reclaimed water permits under RCW 90.46/WAC 173-219, often combined with NPDES/SWD [20][16][29].
  - Section 401 certification pairs with Corps 404/10 for outfalls/crossings; local floodplain/shoreline permits may apply [6][28][26].
- Monitoring and labs: NPDES/SWD permits specify monitoring; lab accreditation rules and exceptions are in WAC 173-216 [20][7]. CSO monitoring expectations are outlined in CSWD [11]. Reclaimed water monitoring is specified by the Purple Book [5].
- Precedence/conflicts: Water Quality Standards (WAC 173-201A) govern and can require limits more stringent than technology-based standards; mixing zones require AKART and must protect uses [23][31][20]. When requirements conflict (e.g., CCC), meet the most restrictive [37]. SERP allows SEPA to satisfy NEPA-like requirements for Ecology-funded projects; EPA retains NEPA oversight for WIFIA [14][2].

Side-by-side comparison (by topic)

Planning
- WAC 173-240
  - Requires Ecology approval of general sewer plans and engineering reports; defines contents and the “no substantial change” standard between report and plans/specs [15][18][35][34].
  - Requires SEPA compliance before Ecology approval; allows phased SEPA [36].
- WAC 173-216
  - Ties permitting to plans reviewed under 173-240; for a given facility, SWD and NPDES are normally in a single permit [7].
- WAC 173-201A
  - Establishes water quality goals that planning and alternatives must meet; may drive more stringent decisions than technology-based norms [23].
- RCW
  - RCW 90.48.110 authorizes Ecology review/approval of plans/reports before construction [31][21].
  - RCW 90.46 sets reclaimed water planning/permitting framework (ART reliability, lead/nonlead roles) [27][29].
- Federal/EPA
  - Ecology is delegated for NPDES; SERP (Ecology) can satisfy NEPA-like review for SRF funding; EPA retains NEPA for WIFIA [2][14].
- WEF/other standards
  - Ecology reviews for reasonable consistency with CSWD and can rely on WEF MOPs, ASCE, APWA, and EPA manuals as additional references [15][22].

Design
- WAC 173-240
  - Plans/specs content and format per CSWD; include design criteria and interim operation plans; CQA plan and O&M manual required [34][24].
- WAC 173-216
  - Not design-specific; permits will reference design for enforceable operating limits [20].
- WAC 173-201A
  - Mixing zones only if AKART applied and protective; may necessitate tighter design to meet criteria at edge of zone [20][31].
- RCW
  - RCW 90.48 AKART requirement; for reclaimed water, RCW 90.46 ART adds reliability beyond AKART [27][20].
- Federal/EPA
  - NPDES requires technology-based plus WQS-based limits; EPA process design manuals are acceptable references [15].
- WEF/other standards
  - CSWD is the primary state design guide; WEF MOPs and ASCE practices are accepted references for design justification [22][15].
- Small systems notes
  - On-site systems: local health (≤3,499 gpd) and DOH LOSS (3,500–100,000 gpd) outside CSWD, though CSWD collection guidance may be referenced; dam safety for embankments ≥10 acre-feet; construction stormwater permit if ≥1 acre disturbed [26][6][28].

Permitting
- WAC 173-240
  - Planning/design approvals prerequisite to construction and support NPDES/SWD permitting [24][31].
- WAC 173-216
  - SWD permits regulate discharges to ground and certain to sewers; include limits, monitoring, and allow combined NPDES/SWD permits; delegation to qualified local programs for sewer discharges [20][31][7].
- WAC 173-201A
  - WQS drive final effluent limits and mixing zone conditions in NPDES/SWD permits [20][31].
- RCW
  - RCW 90.48 governs water pollution control and permitting authority; RCW 90.46 governs reclaimed water permits (may be combined with NPDES/SWD) [20][16].
- Federal/EPA
  - NPDES for surface water discharges; five-year permits with effluent limits/monitoring; Corps 404/10 permits for in-water work; 401 certification by Ecology [20][2][6][28].
- Other/local
  - SEPA required; local shoreline/floodplain/building permits; tribal consultation; construction stormwater general permit [36][26][6].

Monitoring and reporting
- WAC 173-240
  - Planning documents must specify expected effluent and monitoring sufficient to demonstrate compliance; plans and specs include instrumentation and sampling schematics [35][38].
- WAC 173-216
  - Lab accreditation required for submitted monitoring data with limited parameter exceptions; permits include monitoring schedules; modification/revocation provisions [7][31].
- WAC 173-201A
  - Compliance assessed against numeric criteria and protection of designated uses; affects receiving water monitoring and mixing zone evaluations [20][31].
- RCW
  - 90.48 enforcement framework for monitoring-based compliance; reclaimed water monitoring under 90.46/WAC 173-219 [20][5].
- Federal/EPA
  - NPDES monitoring and reporting required; CSO monitoring guidance referenced in CSWD (PSEP protocols) [20][11].
- WEF/other standards
  - CSWD and WEF/EPA manuals serve as technical bases for sampling/monitoring methods accepted by Ecology [15][11].

Cross-connection, reliability, and special topics
- WAC 173-240/CSWD
  - Potable supply CCC and backflow per DOH/ODW and purveyor; meet most restrictive; use air gaps; consider reclaimed effluent for non-potable uses; include CCC devices and coordination [37].
- Reclaimed water (RCW 90.46/WAC 173-219)
  - ART reliability standard; cross-connection control specialist involvement; CCC details in design and as-builts/O&M; lead/nonlead agency coordination [27][4][8][29][32].
- Construction and ancillary permits
  - 401 certification; Construction Stormwater General Permit; dam safety; Corps 404/10; local floodplain/shoreline permits [6][28][26].

Precedence and conflicts
- Water quality standards prevail: If technology-based standards are insufficient, WQS under WAC 173-201A require more stringent permit limits and may drive more protective designs; mixing zones only after AKART and if protective [23][31][20].
- Single/combined permits: Ecology may issue combined NPDES/SWD and include reclaimed water conditions as separate sections; WAC 173-216-140 favors single permit documents where both programs apply [16][7][20].
- Most restrictive rule: For cross-connection/backflow and similar public health protections, comply with the most restrictive applicable requirement (purveyor, DOH code, CSWD) [37].
- SEPA/NEPA/SERP: SEPA is required for plan/report adoption; SERP lets Ecology use SEPA to meet NEPA-like reviews for SRF; EPA retains NEPA for WIFIA—federal requirements control where applicable [36][14][2].
- Jurisdiction for small systems: On-site systems are under local health/DOH (not CSWD), but discharges to waters of the state still must meet RCW 90.48 protections; reclaimed water uses remain under RCW 90.46 where applicable [26][27].

Conclusion
For small Washington wastewater systems, plan and design under WAC 173-240 with CSWD consistency, permit under NPDES (surface water), SWD (groundwater), and reclaimed water rules as applicable, and monitor under permit-specific schedules with WAC 173-216 lab standards. Water Quality Standards (WAC 173-201A) set the controlling endpoint; where requirements conflict, apply the most protective/controlling standard. Use CSWD as the primary design basis, supplemented by WEF/ASCE/EPA references recognized by Ecology [15][22][23][20][31].
