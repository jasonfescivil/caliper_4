assistant: Master outline — Small-community (<1,000) WA wastewater treatment feasibility (engineering) report

0. Cover, executive summary, and report compliance statement
- Purpose: Identify owner/representative; summarize need, scope, findings; certify the report is “sufficiently complete” to develop plans without substantial changes.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(a) owner/representative; 060(2) sufficiently complete [7]
- Orange Book: Ecology review purpose and conformance with design standards [9]
- Federal: unknown

1. Regulatory framework overview (state, local, tribal, federal)
- Purpose: Define applicable permits/standards and how the project will comply.
- WAC 173-240: Chapter scope and submittal framework [2]; SEPA 173-802 [2]; WQS 173-201A (criteria; mixing zones; AKART) [2]; NPDES 173-220 [2]; GWQS 173-200 [2]; SWD 173-216 (incl. reclaimed water; small-system thresholds) [2]
- Orange Book: G1-1.5 local permits—building, shoreline, floodplain, air quality [8]; tribal consultation requirements [8]
- Federal: Clean Water Act NPDES framework (implemented via 173-220) [2]; NEPA/SERP requirements for CWSRF projects; 40 CFR Part 35 Subpart K (SERP) [10]

2. Planning basis and conformance with General Sewer Plan (GSP)
- Purpose: Confirm GSP coverage; define project need and planning sequence.
- WAC 173-240: 050 (GSP; if not current, consult Ecology re: elements to include in engineering report) [7]
- Orange Book: Planning sequence and approvals (GSP → Engineering Report → Plans/Specs) [6]; G1-3 content/objective of GSP [10]
- Federal: SERP/NEPA alternative analysis/public participation expectations for funded projects [10]

3. Project description, location, and service area
- Purpose: Describe where, what, and why; map present and proposed service area and project features.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(b) project description, location and service area maps [7]
- Orange Book: G1-3.2 mapping content (system layouts, boundaries, water bodies, outfalls) [10]
- Federal: unknown

4. Small-community context and resources
- Purpose: Address small-system considerations (consultant selection, rate setting, financing).
- WAC 173-240: General engineering/planning applicability [2]
- Orange Book: G3 Special Considerations for Small Community Systems; G3-2 Identifying and Securing Resources (consultant; rates/connection fees; financing) [1]
- Federal: CWSRF program as a potential funding source subject to SERP/NEPA-like review [10]

5. Existing wastewater flows and quality
- Purpose: Characterize current flows and loadings; establish design basis.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(c) present quantity/quality (flow, BOD5, TSS, constituents of concern; at least 1 year of data; lab accreditation; metering) [7]
- Orange Book: Emphasis on credible data; variability; return streams locations [7]
- Federal: unknown

6. Future wastewater projections
- Purpose: Project 20-year flows/loads and sources to size facilities.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(c) future wastes (normally 20 years) [7]
- Orange Book: Use GSP population and land-use data; OFM and local planning inputs [7][10]
- Federal: unknown

7. Infiltration/inflow (I/I), overflows, and bypasses
- Purpose: Quantify I/I; analyze SSO/CSO risks; propose mitigation/controls.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(j) I/I, overflows, bypasses; one-year baseline, mapping, mitigation, no increased SSO/CSO frequency [5]
- Orange Book: Consider equalization, rehab, and impacts on technology selection [5]
- Federal: unknown

8. Industrial waste and pretreatment needs
- Purpose: Identify significant industrial sources; assess treatability; define pretreatment.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(k) special provisions and pretreatment for industrial wastes [5]
- Orange Book: Reference treatability to protect processes and biosolids quality [5]
- Federal: unknown

9. Receiving water or groundwater characterization and standards
- Purpose: Describe receiving environment; determine limiting criteria; mixing zone needs.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(e) receiving water description; data sources (EIM, WQX, USGS, NOAA, FERC); 7Q10 for streams; marine profiles; WET rule reference; shellfish bed considerations; hydrogeologic study for groundwater [3]
- Orange Book: Guidance/methodology expectations for mixing zone evaluations [3]
- Federal: EPA Technical Support Document for Water Quality–based Toxics Control (for WQ-based limits/mixing analyses) [5]; National Shellfish Sanitation Program Model Ordinance guidance (FDA) for prohibited areas near WWTPs [3]

10. Regulatory standards and AKART determination
- Purpose: Show how water quality standards will be met (at end-of-pipe or mixing zone) and define AKART.
- WAC 173-201A (criteria; mixing zones; AKART basis) [2]; 173-221 (AKART for domestic wastewater = secondary treatment; may require additional treatment) [2]
- Orange Book: Acronyms/definitions (AKART) context [4]
- Federal: Clean Water Act basis for NPDES limits (implemented via state program) [2]

11. Alternatives analysis (including No Action)
- Purpose: Identify feasible alternatives; evaluate and select preferred alternative.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(f) treatment/disposal type; alternatives, including no action; conservation options [3]
- Orange Book: Evaluate feasibility—environmental impacts, cost effectiveness (present worth), technical complexity/certified operator availability, risk, legal/regulatory barriers [3]; small-community technologies context [1]
- Federal: SERP/NEPA requires consideration of alternatives with public participation [10]

12. Site selection and environmental review
- Purpose: Compare candidate sites; address environmental, cultural, and geotechnical risks; define SEPA/SERP steps.
- WAC 173-240: Submittal/approval framework; SEPA process applicability [2]
- Orange Book: SEPA purpose and timing; public involvement [2]; site assessment—tsunami inundation risk, DAHP/tribal historic properties consultation, soils analyses to avoid changed conditions [5]
- Federal: NEPA/SERP elements for CWSRF projects (interdisciplinary review, documentation, public notice, alternatives) [10]

13. Selected alternative — preliminary design basis
- Purpose: Establish preliminary unit processes and performance standards to a level enabling plans/specs development without substantial changes.
- WAC 173-240: 060(2) sufficiently complete [7]; 060(3)(f) selected process rationale [3]
- Orange Book: Ecology review and design standard conformance; common engineering practice [9]
- Federal: EPA design criteria references for mechanical/electrical/fluid reliability (as general reference) [1]

14. Hydraulic profile and process flow diagrams
- Purpose: Show all liquid/solids/recycle streams; sampling/flow monitoring points; develop critical-condition hydraulic profiles.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(i) flow diagram and hydraulic profile [5]
- Orange Book: Include site layout/topography; profiles for high/low plant and receiving water conditions [5]
- Federal: unknown

15. Outfall and disposal analysis
- Purpose: Locate/design outfall/diffuser; demonstrate compliance at end-of-pipe or mixing zone under critical conditions.
- WAC 173-240: 060(3)(l) detailed outfall analysis; consistency with Ecology mixing zone guidance; modeling (Visual PLUMES/CORMIX); all critical flows/loadings [5]
- Orange Book: Receiving water data needs and modeling approach [3][5]
- Federal: EPA Technical Support Document for WQ-based toxics control (basis for analysis) [5]

16. Operations, staffing, ownership, and O&M documentation
- Purpose: Define operator certification needs, O&M approach, and public ownership commitments.
- WAC 173-240: Chapter 173-240 requirements for O&M manual; certified operator; public ownership [2]
- Orange Book: Review/approval process for O&M manuals [9]
- Federal: unknown

17. Permitting and approvals roadmap
- Purpose: Identify all permits/approvals and cycles; outline submittal timing.
- WAC 173-220 NPDES (5-year permit cycle; limits, monitoring, special conditions; application/renewal/public notice) [2]; 173-216 SWD for groundwater/municipal system discharges; exclusions/DOH thresholds [2]; 173-240 submittals (GSP → Eng. Report → Plans/Specs) [6]
- Orange Book: Local permits—building; Shoreline permit; Floodplain development; air quality authority [8]; tribal coordination [8]
- Federal: NEPA/SERP for CWSRF; public participation elements [10]

18. Funding, affordability, and rate/fee strategy
- Purpose: Define financing plan; evaluate user rates and connection fees appropriate for small communities.
- WAC 173-240: General planning framework [2]
- Orange Book: G3-2 Identifying and Securing Resources; G3-2.2 rates/connection fees; G1-2.8 funding eligibility and Ecology Combined Funding Program [1][10]
- Federal: Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF) program context (SERP/NEPA-like review) [10]

19. Public involvement plan
- Purpose: Describe SEPA/SERP-aligned public notification, meetings, and comment opportunities.
- WAC 173-240: SEPA applicability and timing; public involvement via SEPA early in planning [2]
- Orange Book: SERP reliance on SEPA; expanded public participation to align with NEPA [10]
- Federal: NEPA public participation standards as applied via SERP [10]

20. Implementation plan and schedule
- Purpose: Provide phasing, milestones for Ecology approvals (GSP, Eng. Report, Plans/Specs), permitting, bidding, and construction.
- WAC 173-240: Submittal and approval sequence [6]
- Orange Book: Figure G1-1 sequence of planning, design, and construction [6]
- Federal: unknown

21. Appendices
- A. Data and analyses
  - Purpose: Provide supporting datasets and methods.
  - WAC 173-240: Receiving water data sources (EIM, WQX, USGS, NOAA, FERC); 7Q10, marine profiles; toxic pollutant scan/WET considerations; groundwater hydrogeologic study minimums [3]
  - Orange Book: Calibration/credible data requirements; lab accreditation; flow meter calibration [7]
  - Federal: EPA TSD (WQ-based toxics); National Shellfish Sanitation Program guidance link [3][5]
- B. Maps and figures (service area, system layouts, outfalls)
  - WAC 173-240: Mapping requirements [7][10]
- C. Abbreviations and definitions
  - Orange Book: Acronyms; glossary reference [4]
- D. References
  - Orange Book: General references for small systems and design (EPA design criteria; WEF odor control MOP; ASCE/ISA/Sanks/Chow/Isco) [1]
