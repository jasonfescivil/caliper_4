assistant: I/I Plan for Small Washington Systems

1) Purpose and Regulatory Framework
- Purpose: quantify and reduce RDII to meet permit and capacity goals, following EPA SSES concepts, WEF field methods, and Washington standards.
- References:
  - WA Criteria for Sewage Works Design (CSWD, “Orange Book”): general engineering/plan contents and O&M/recordkeeping requirements [DOE CSWD G1-4.2, May 2023; p.57, G1-4.2.2 Contents of Plans; and B. Plans for Sewers] [23]; instrumentation/control and safety frameworks [G2-6.3, G2-6.4, G2-7] [17]; purpose of manual [p.1] [22].
  - WAC 173-240 review standards and submittals (plans/specs; O&M manual with monitoring/recordkeeping) [p.4, WAC 173-240-040] [38]; [p.9, WAC 173-240-070 and 173-240-075/080] [50]; [p.15, WAC 173-240-140] [41].
  - EPA: CMOM guidance; SSOAP; SSES reference (EPA-600/2-77/017d) listed in WEF references [WEF FD-6, p.47–48, 7.0 REFERENCES; EPA items] [28][25]; NPDES monitoring/recordkeeping framework [2010 Permit Writers’ Manual, Ch. 8] [33].
  - Ecology I/I analysis requirements as used in small WA systems (example permit condition and use of monitoring records) [AWWA PPWTF I/I Assessment, p.11, Section 1] [21].

2) SSES Phases (overview and methods to be used)
- Phase 1 – Planning and Scoping
  - Define goals, scope, accuracy, schedule; use existing records (WRRF flows, pump on–off, rainfall) [WEF FD-6, p.5, 2.1; p.126, 2.1/2.2] [14][39].
  - Establish monitoring scope for I/I and CMOM/SSO needs [WEF FD-6, p.5, 2.3] [46].
- Phase 2 – Flow Monitoring and Rainfall
  - Deploy wastewater flow monitors and rainfall gauges to capture dry- and wet-weather responses [WEF FD-6, p.2, 4.4.7 Wastewater Flow Monitoring] [1]; [p.126, ideal program for dry/wet conditions] [39].
  - Monitor for sufficient wet-weather period per WEF guidance [WEF FD-6, p.5, 2.5 Duration of Wet Weather Monitoring] [10].
- Phase 3 – System Inspections and Field Tests
  - Manhole/cleanout visual inspections; inspected pipeline/manholes listing [WEF FD-6, p.2, 4.4.1–4.4.3] [1].
  - Smoke testing to identify inflow sources [WEF FD-6, p.3, 2.6.1 Smoke Testing] [2][4].
  - Dyed water flooding and leak quantification; include public notification and controlled flooding of storm features; coordinate with CCTV [WEF FD-6, p.77, 4.4.6; p.212–213, 5.3.1–5.3.2 and Fig. 5.11a–b] [35][15].
  - Closed-circuit television inspection to document defects [WEF FD-6, p.2, 4.4.5] [1].
  - Night flow isolation to screen for groundwater infiltration at minimum flows [WEF FD-6, p.78, 4.4.8] [12].
- Phase 4 – Analysis and RDII Correlation
  - Correlate rainfall and flow to quantify RDII by basin; use WEF hydrology/wet-weather flow generation guidance for separate and combined systems [WEF FD-6, p.6, 3.5.4; 3.5.8; 3.5.9; 3.5.10] [16][36][20][11].
  - Document RDII removal benefits and verification approach [WEF FD-6, p.16, 3.2] [45].
  - Use EPA SSOAP/SSES references for analytical framework (as cited in WEF FD-6 references) [WEF FD-6, p.47–48, 7.0 REFERENCES] [28][25].
- Phase 5 – Rehabilitation Planning and Reporting
  - Develop prioritized corrective actions and capital plan; integrate with CSWD plan/O&M requirements [DOE CSWD G1-4.2 plan contents] [23]; WAC O&M manual monitoring and recordkeeping [p.9, 173-240-080(g),(h)] [50].
  - For NPDES facilities, compile I/I evaluation/report per permit and Ecology guidance (use monitoring records; report and plan if >15% increase) [AWWA PPWTF I/I Assessment, p.11, bullets 2–4] [21].

3) Monitoring Periods and Locations
- Periods:
  - Dry-weather baseline and multiple wet-weather events per WEF monitoring duration guidance [WEF FD-6, p.5, 2.5] [10] and to characterize “different weather and tidal conditions” [WEF FD-6, p.126] [39].
- Locations:
  - Key basins and potential bottlenecks based on traced system markup and known problem areas [WEF FD-6, p.126, 2.1–2.3] [39][46].
  - Include upstream/downstream of pump stations and storage/force mains as needed [WEF FD-6, p.126, item 6] [39].
- Monitoring and reporting framework should follow EPA permit monitoring principles (location, frequency, sample collection, and recordkeeping) [2010 EPA PWM, Ch. 8 (8.1–8.5)] [33][34].

4) Field Methods (procedures)
- Smoke Testing
  - Prepare equipment and setup per WEF [WEF FD-6, p.9, 5.2.4] [8].
  - Conduct tests and document with QA/QC protocols [WEF FD-6, p.9, 5.2.7] [6]; method reference [WEF FD-6, p.3, 2.6.1] [2][4].
- Dyed Water Flooding and Leak Quantification
  - Public notification to utilities/regulators before dye use [WEF FD-6, p.212, 5.3.1] [15].
  - Plug storm segments and add dyed water (≤ half diameter), monitor downstream sanitary manholes for dye presence; manage safety of inflatable plugs to prevent flooding [WEF FD-6, p.212–213, 5.3.2] [15].
  - Quantify leaks with portable weirs/hydrostatic devices/container timed tests, and coordinate with CCTV [WEF FD-6, p.77, 4.4.6] [35].
- Manhole/Cleanout Inspection and CCTV
  - Inventory inspected lines/manholes and perform CCTV to document defects/inflow points [WEF FD-6, p.2, 4.4.1–4.4.5] [1].
- Night Flow Isolation
  - Subdivide basins; measure minimum flows to infer groundwater infiltration hotspots [WEF FD-6, p.78, 4.4.8] [12].
- Safety and Control
  - Follow CSWD safety requirements (confined spaces, fall protection, electrical safety, etc.) [DOE CSWD G2-7] [17].

5) Rainfall–Flow Correlation and RDII Analysis
- Use concurrent rainfall and flow time series to quantify RDII response by basin; apply WEF hydrology and wet-weather generation guidance [WEF FD-6, p.6, 3.5.4; 3.5.8; 3.5.10] [16][36][11].
- Document methods and results for RDII removal verification [WEF FD-6, p.16, 3.2] [45].
- EPA SSES/SSOAP references (as cited) provide accepted analytical approaches [WEF FD-6, p.47–48, 7.0 REFERENCES] [28][25].

6) QA/QC and Documentation
- Smoke testing documentation and QA/QC procedures [WEF FD-6, p.9, 5.2.7] [6].
- Equipment setup and standardized procedures for repeatability [WEF FD-6, p.9, 5.2.4] [8].
- Maintain monitoring records and keep analytical/recordkeeping per EPA Ch. 8 [2010 EPA PWM, 8.4–8.5] [33].
- O&M manual to include sampling/monitoring requirements and recordkeeping forms [WAC 173-240-080(4)(g),(h), p.9] [50].

7) Deliverables
- Monitoring Plan: locations, duration, instruments, QA/QC (per [WEF FD-6, p.5, 2.1–2.3; p.5, 2.5]) [14][46][10].
- Field Work Packages: smoke test procedures and forms; dye test plan and public notification log; CCTV logs; manhole inspection forms (dye form example referenced) [WEF FD-6, p.212–213 (Fig. 5.11b)] [15]; [WEF FD-6, p.9, 5.2.7] [6].
- Data and Analysis Report: dry/wet weather flows; rainfall–flow correlation; RDII by basin; night flow isolation results; prioritized defects [WEF FD-6, p.16, 3.2; p.6, 3.5.x] [45][16][36][11][20][42].
- Rehabilitation Plan and Schedule aligned with CSWD plan/O&M requirements [DOE CSWD G1-4.2.2; WAC 173-240-070(2)] [23][50].
- O&M Manual updates covering monitoring, lab procedures, and recordkeeping [WAC 173-240-080(4)(g),(h)] [50].
- Submittals prepared to be reasonably consistent with CSWD per WAC 173-240-040 [p.4] [38].

Field Checklist (minimum)
- General/Safety
  - Confined space entry plan; gas monitoring; fall protection; electrical safety [DOE CSWD G2-7] [17].
  - Traffic control as applicable.
- Flow Monitoring
  - Site verification; sensor installation; level/velocity zero checks; time sync; data logging start; photo documentation [WEF FD-6, p.126, 2.1–2.3] [39].
- Rainfall
  - Gauge location verification; logging start; data integrity checks [2010 EPA PWM, 8.1 monitoring setup context] [33].
- Manhole/Cleanout Inspections
  - Asset ID; condition ratings; signs of inflow/infiltration; photos; GPS [WEF FD-6, p.2, 4.4.1–4.4.3] [1].
- Smoke Testing
  - Equipment setup per WEF; blower/smoke condition; downstream notification; observations logged; defects mapped; QA/QC signoff [WEF FD-6, p.9, 5.2.4; p.9, 5.2.7; p.3, 2.6.1] [8][6][2].
- Dyed Water Flooding
  - Public/regulatory notification complete [WEF FD-6, p.212, 5.3.1] [15].
  - Plugs installed; dye mix rate; fill level control (≤ 50% diameter); monitor sanitary manholes; safety checks; data sheet completed; coordinate CCTV if needed [WEF FD-6, p.212–213, 5.3.2; p.77, 4.4.6] [15][35].
- CCTV
  - Pre- and post-cleaning status; PACP-style log if used; video file naming; defect coding [WEF FD-6, p.2, 4.4.5] [1].
- Night Flow Isolation
  - Basin isolation plan; minimum-flow readings; documentation of isolation points; compare to baseline [WEF FD-6, p.78, 4.4.8] [12].
- Documentation
  - Daily reports; photos; GIS markups; calibration records; chain of custody for data [WEF FD-6, p.9, 5.2.7] [6]; O&M recordkeeping [WAC 173-240-080(4)(h)] [50].

Minimum Instrumentation Specifications/Features
- Wastewater Flow Monitors
  - Capable of continuous recording at key locations to capture dry- and wet-weather hydrographs and minimum night flows for isolation [WEF FD-6, p.126 (dry/wet data need); p.78 (night flow isolation)] [39][12].
  - Deployed per a monitoring scope that matches project objectives and required data accuracy (documented in plan) [WEF FD-6, p.5, 2.1] [14].
  - Integration with historical database/SCADA as applicable to support data management [DOE CSWD G2-6.4.6(D) Historical Database Management] [17].
- Rain Gauges
  - Continuous logging sufficient to correlate rainfall and sewer response per WEF wet-weather monitoring and hydrology sections [WEF FD-6, p.5, 2.5; p.6, 3.5.8] [10][36].
- Leak Quantification Tools
  - Portable weirs/hydrostatic devices/container-time tests for dyed-water leak quantification [WEF FD-6, p.77, 4.4.6] [35].
- General I&C and Reliability
  - Comply with applicable codes and good practice for instrumentation/control systems and reliability [DOE CSWD G2-6.3/G2-6.4; reference to EPA reliability classifications] [24][17].
- Documentation and Plans
  - Provide instrumentation/control schematics and operating procedures in plans/specs per WAC/CSWD [WAC 173-240-140(2)(g),(h), p.15] [41]; [DOE CSWD G1-4.2.2 contents] [23].

Notes on Rainfall-Derived I/I Documentation
- Use WEF guidance for documenting RDII removal [WEF FD-6, p.16, 3.2] [45] and hydrology of separate/combined systems [WEF FD-6, p.6, 3.5.4; 3.5.9; 3.5.10] [16][20][11].
- Align monitoring/analysis and reporting with EPA monitoring/recordkeeping expectations [2010 EPA PWM, Ch. 8] [33] and Ecology permit-driven I/I evaluation/reporting [AWWA PPWTF, p.11] [21].

All sections above are grounded in the cited sources and should be adapted to local system constraints and permit conditions.
