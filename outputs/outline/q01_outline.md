Below is a master outline for a small‑community (<1,000 population) Washington State wastewater treatment feasibility (engineering) report. For each report section I give: (1) short purpose, (2) the controlling WAC citation (as used in the Orange Book), (3) the matching Orange Book chapter/section and page label, and (4) federal references or other guidance cited in the Orange Book where applicable. I used ONLY the provided source material and quote the exact WAC subsections shown in that material.

Master outline (sections numbered for report structure)

0. Title page, cover letter, owner’s certification
- Purpose: Identify owner, authorized representative and certify authority to proceed.
- Required citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(3)(a).
- Orange Book: “Minimum Information Required” — WAC 173‑240‑060(3)(a) (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3].
- Federal refs: N/A.

1. Executive summary
- Purpose: Concise summary of existing conditions, preferred alternative, costs, schedule, permits and funding needs.
- Citation anchor: WAC 173‑240 (engineering report overall requirements) — refer to G1-4 engineering report objectives and submission sequence.
- Orange Book: G1 General Engineering Requirements; G1-4 Engineering Report objective and sequence (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.12 and p.41) [4], [10].
- Federal refs: CWSRF funding guidance (see SERP/NEPA note in Section 13) [10 p.41].

2. Project description and service area maps
- Purpose: Describe project location, service area, ownership, need and relationship to approved general sewer plan.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(3)(b) (project description, location map, service area).
- Orange Book: “Minimum Information Required” — WAC 173‑240‑060(3)(b) (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3]; general sewer plan consistency: G1-3 and G1-2 sequence (G1-3.2 and G1-2.2) (p.33, p.31) [6], [8].
- Federal refs: If project receives federal funding, NEPA applies (see Section 13) [10 p.41].

3. Present and projected flows and wastewater characterization
- Purpose: Document present and future (design period, typically 20 years) flows and pollutant loadings (flow, BOD5, TSS), diurnal/seasonal variation, and I/I baseline.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(3)(c).
- Orange Book: Explanation of WAC 173‑240‑060(c) — one full year of flow/loading minimum; estimate future (normally 20 years); lab accreditation and metering calibration requirements (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50 and explanatory paragraphs) [3].
- Cross references: I/I analysis details also mapped to WAC 173‑240‑060(j) (see Section 7 below) [3].
- Federal refs: N/A.

4. Receiving water description and applicable water quality standards
- Purpose: Describe receiving water (location, classification, 7Q10 or tidal info), existing water quality data, designated uses, limiting criteria and need for mixing zone analysis.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(e) — “A description of the receiving water, applicable water quality standards, and how water quality standards will be met at the boundary of any applicable dilution zone.”
- Orange Book: Detailed explanation of WAC 173‑240‑060(e) including required metrics (river mile, latitude/longitude, 7Q10, MLLW depth, current/salinity/temperature profiles), and toxic pollutant / WET considerations (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3]; SEPA / AKART / secondary treatment context (G3-1.1.7 and Chapter cross‑references) (G3 section, p.179) [2].
- Federal refs/guidance: NPDES permitting program (federal/state) and mixing zone/WET implications (WAC 173‑220 / NPDES) — see G3-1.1.3 and mixing‑zone/AKART discussion (Orange Book G3 and G1 references) (p.179) [2]; EPA technical guidance referenced for outfall/mixing zone (see Section 10) [6].

5. Regulatory and permitting summary (state and federal)
- Purpose: List all required permits/approvals (Ecology engineering approval, NPDES/State Waste Discharge, SEPA, Shoreline, County building, floodplain, tribal consultations).
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240 (general submission/approval), and specific laws referenced in Orange Book G1-1 and G3 (e.g., Chapters 173‑221, 173‑201A, 173‑220, 173‑216, 173‑240, 173‑802 as listed).
- Orange Book: G1-1 Agreements and Applicable Permits; G3-1 Regulatory Framework (list of WAC chapters) (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.12 and G3 chapter listing p.175) [4], [1].
- Federal refs: NEPA/CWSRF SERP (if federal funds used) — see G1 SERP/CWSRF discussion (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.41) [10]; NPDES program (G3-1.1.3) [2].

6. Alternatives analysis (including no‑action)
- Purpose: Identify and evaluate feasible alternatives (conventional, advanced, lagoons, land application, regional treatment, no action) and select preferred alternative based on environmental impact, present worth, technical complexity, operator availability, project risk and regulatory/financial constraints.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(f) — “The type of treatment process proposed … and a discussion of the alternatives evaluated and the reasons they are unacceptable.”
- Orange Book: Explanation of 173‑240‑060(f) — alternatives evaluation criteria and requirement for a no‑action alternative (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3]; small community special considerations in G3 (G3-2 and G3-1) (p.175) [1].
- Federal refs: CAA/NEPA SERP elements for alternative consideration when federal funding involved (G1/SERP) [10 p.41].

7. Infiltration & inflow (I/I) and hydraulic analysis of collection system
- Purpose: Assess I/I extent, seasonal variability, flows from combined sewers, provide monitoring data and propose remedies (rehab, equalization, sizing).
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(j) — “A discussion of infiltration and inflow problems, overflows and bypasses, and proposed corrections and controls.”
- Orange Book: Explanation of 173‑240‑060(j) — one year of testing recommended, evaluate system age/characteristics, identify SSO/CSO locations and impacts, relate to facility design (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3]; cross reference to Section C1‑7 for I/I guidance (Orange Book) [3].
- Federal refs: CSO control plan consistency per Chapter 173‑245 WAC as noted in Orange Book [3].

8. Industrial wastes / pretreatment requirements
- Purpose: Identify significant industrial users, characterize industrial discharges, evaluate pretreatment needs and potential interference or biosolids impacts.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(k).
- Orange Book: Explanation of 173‑240‑060(k) — treatability reference, pretreatment extent, biosolids suitability (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3].
- Federal refs: Pretreatment oversight delegation to local governments (Orange Book G1-2 and G1-5 references) [10], [4].

9. Flow diagrams, process schematics, and hydraulic profiles
- Purpose: Provide schematic process flow diagrams (liquid & solids), return/recycle flows, sampling/monitoring locations, scaled site layout with topography, and hydraulic profiles for critical conditions.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(i).
- Orange Book: Explanation of 173‑240‑060(i) — develop flow diagrams for alternates; include schematic showing returns/recycles and sampling points; scaled layout and hydraulic profiles at high/low flows (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3].
- Federal refs: N/A.

10. Outfall design and receiving water impact (mixing zone / diffuser analysis)
- Purpose: Provide detailed outfall/diffuser location and hydraulic/dilution analysis demonstrating compliance with water quality standards at mixing zone boundaries or demonstrating no mixing zone required.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(l) and receiving water requirements under 173‑201A.
- Orange Book: Explanation of 173‑240‑060(l) — detailed outfall analysis, consistency with Ecology’s Guidance for Conducting Mixing Zone Analyses and EPA “Technical Support Document for Water Quality‑based Toxics Control”; encourages calibrated plume models (Visual PLUMES or CORMIX) and inclusion of all critical flows (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.33 / p.50) [6], [3].
- Federal refs: EPA Technical Support Document referenced in Orange Book (mixing zone/toxics control) [6].

11. Treatment unit process selection, design basis and preliminary sizing
- Purpose: Present selected treatment process, design criteria, preliminary unit sizing, basis of design (flows, loadings, removal efficiencies) and operator/maintenance considerations.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(f) (treatment type) and WAC 173‑240‑060(2) “sufficiently complete” design basis.
- Orange Book: 173‑240‑060(f) explanation and 060(2) “Sufficiently Complete” definition — report must allow independent engineer to produce construction drawings without substantial reevaluation (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3].
- Federal refs: Operator certification / availability consideration per Orange Book G1 discussions (G1-4.1 and G1-1 sections) [10], [4].

12. Site assessment, geotechnical and cultural resources
- Purpose: Document site selection process, soils analyses sufficient for design, inundation/tsunami/flood risk, and cultural resources consultation (DAHP, tribal historic preservation).
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240 submission/site requirements (referenced in Orange Book G1-2.5 and site assessment guidance).
- Orange Book: Site assessment guidance including inundation/tsunami risk, soils analyses and requirement to consult DAHP and tribal offices (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.53) [5]; additional geotechnical expectations are part of project‑specific planning (G1 sections) [10].
- Federal refs: NEPA/Section 106 considerations if federal funds/actions (see Section 13) [10].

13. Environmental review (SEPA / NEPA / SERP) and public involvement
- Purpose: Document required environmental review, public notice/participation, SEPA checklist timing, and SERP/NEPA obligations if federal funding is involved.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑802 (SEPA) referenced in Orange Book G3-1.1.7 and WAC 173‑240 environmental review requirements.
- Orange Book: G3-1.1.7 SEPA discussion (State Environmental Policy Act) (G3 chapter p.179) [2]; G1 SERP / NEPA and Ecology’s approved SERP program and NEPA‑like state review for CWSRF (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.41) [10].
- Federal refs: EPA CWSRF NEPA rules and SERP approved program (Orange Book citation) [10].

14. Operation, maintenance and staffing (including O&M manual outline)
- Purpose: Describe required operator certification, staffing plan, training, preliminary O&M manual content and parts/supplies.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060 and Orange Book G1-4 operation & maintenance manual requirements.
- Orange Book: G1-4 Operation and Maintenance Manual sections and requirement that a certified operator be identified (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.12 and p.41) [4], [10].
- Federal refs: Funding eligibility considerations (operator availability often a funding criterion) [10].

15. Cost estimate, life‑cycle and funding strategy (rates, fees, grants/loans)
- Purpose: Present opinion of probable cost (capital + contingencies), present worth/lifecycle cost comparison between alternatives, and funding strategy including SRF/grants and rate/fee evaluation.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240 requirement that engineering reports include project costs and financing discussion (covered under general engineering report expectations).
- Orange Book: G3-2 Identifying and Securing Resources — Hiring a consultant, Identifying Financial Resources, Evaluating and Setting User Rates and Connection Fees (G3 chapter p.175) [1]; funding eligibility and CWSRF references (G1-2.8, p.41) [10].
- Federal refs: CWSRF program rules and SERP (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.41) [10].

16. Monitoring, compliance and sampling plan
- Purpose: Describe proposed influent/effluent sampling locations, frequency, types of samples (grab/composite), laboratory requirements, and how monitoring supports permit compliance.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(i) (sampling locations) and (e) (toxics/WET considerations).
- Orange Book: Explanations for sampling location representativeness, Ecology‑accredited labs and meter calibration expectations (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3]; WET testing discussion under receiving water section (p.50) [3].
- Federal refs: NPDES/WET rules referenced (WET rule, Chapter 173‑205 WAC) per Orange Book [3].

17. Construction phasing, plan of interim operation and risk analysis
- Purpose: Phasing, interim operation plan during construction, constructability issues, potential for “changed site conditions” and contingency / risk mitigation.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240 required plan of interim operation in plans/specs (referenced in Orange Book G1-4.2 and G1-4.3).
- Orange Book: Plan of Interim Operation and Construction QA expectations (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.41 and related plan sections) [10].
- Federal refs: Funding/QA requirements for CWA grants/loans (SERP/CWSRF references) [10].

18. Appendices (data, lab reports, monitoring logs, calculations, model runs, maps, public comments)
- Purpose: Provide raw data and supporting documentation: flow/loads time series, lab reports from Ecology‑accredited labs, geotech reports, plume model outputs (Visual PLUMES/CORMIX runs), public meeting records.
- WAC citation: WAC 173‑240‑060(e) (include data appendices) and 060(3) overall minimum info.
- Orange Book: Guidance to include site monitoring data in appendices and that engineering report be “sufficiently complete” to allow plans/specs development (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3].
- Federal refs: EPA model guidance referenced for outfall/mixing zone (Visual PLUMES/CORMIX; EPA Technical Support Document) [6].

19. References and list of acronyms / glossary
- Purpose: Cite all standards, manuals and regulatory references used; include glossary of terms and acronyms.
- Orange Book: G2 references (e.g., ASCE, ISCO, EPA publications) and the manual’s abbreviation lists (Criteria for Sewage Works Design; Abbreviations section p.12 & list pages) [1], [4].
- Federal refs: EPA publications referenced in relevant sections (mixing zone/TSD; NEPA/CWSRF guidance) [6], [10].

Notes on minimum/report completeness and small‑community considerations
- “Sufficiently complete” requirement: The engineering report must be sufficiently complete so plans/specs can be developed without substantial changes — WAC 173‑240‑060(2) (General Engineering Requirements – May 2023, p.50) [3].
- Small community guidance: Use G3 special considerations for small communities (hiring consultants, setting rates, regulatory framework) — Orange Book G3 (Identifying and Securing Resources; Regulatory Framework) (G3 chapter p.175 & p.179) [1], [2].
- NPDES/AKART: For surface water discharges NPDES permits, mixing zones, and AKART (secondary treatment for domestic wastewater) issues must be addressed per G3 and WAC chapters referenced (Orange Book G3-1.1.3 & G3-1.1.7, p.179) [2].
