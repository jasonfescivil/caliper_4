assistant: Infiltration and Inflow (I/I) Investigation and Control Plan for Small Washington Systems

1) Purpose and regulatory framework
- Objective: Achieve total containment of sanitary wastes and maximum exclusion of I/I in design, operation, and rehabilitation of sewers (DOE Criteria for Sewage Works Design, “Orange Book,” C1-1.3 Design, p.203) [17]; ensure consistency with public health and water quality objectives (Orange Book Purpose of Manual, p.1) [21].
- Submittals and review: Ecology reviews engineering reports, plans/specs, and O&M manuals for domestic wastewater facilities for permit compliance and consistency with the Orange Book per WAC 173-240-040 (WAC 173-240, Review standards, p.4) [40]; sewer plan contents per G1-4.2.2 and Plans for Sewers (Orange Book G1-4.2.2, p.57) [22].
- Permitting context: NPDES permits specify monitoring and reporting; nearly all municipal WWTPs discharging to surface water require an individual NPDES permit (Orange Book G1-1.5, p.29) [35]. Monitoring and reporting conditions are established in EPA’s NPDES Permit Writers’ Manual, Chapter 8 (2010 EPA PWM, p.7) [34].
- Small-community considerations: See Orange Book G3 Special Considerations for Small Community Systems (DOE, G3 chapter, p.175) [4].

2) SSES approach and phases
- Reference framework: EPA Handbook, Sewer System Infrastructure Analysis and Rehabilitation (SSES) (EPA, 1991; cited in EPA Infiltration/Inflow Review, p.28) [45].
- WEF technology applications to include in SSES: manhole/cleanout visual inspection; listing of inspected pipelines/manholes; smoke testing; CCTV; dyed-water flooding/leak quantification; wastewater flow monitoring; night flow isolation (WEF FD-6, §4.4, p.2) [1]; night flow isolation method (WEF FD-6, §4.4.8, p.78) [11].
- Systematic program: Apply a comprehensive, cyclical system study to identify and rehabilitate multiple, interrelated issues; measure success by post-rehabilitation performance (WEF FD-6, §9.0 Summary, p.90) [50].
- Phases
  1. Planning and scoping (flow monitoring project; monitoring needs for I/I, SSO, CMOM; use existing records) (WEF FD-6, §2.1–2.3, p.5; p.126–127) [13] [47] [41].
  2. Flow and rainfall monitoring and rainfall scope definition (WEF FD-6, §2.11 Setting a Rainfall Scope, p.5) [9].
  3. Field investigations: manhole inspections; smoke testing (WEF FD-6, §2.6.1, p.3; §5.2.4–5.2.7, p.206) [2] [5]; dyed-water flooding and leak quantification (WEF FD-6, §4.4.6, p.77; dyed-water procedures and forms, p.212) [38] [14]; CCTV (WEF FD-6, §4.4, p.2) [1]; night flow isolation (WEF FD-6, §4.4.8, p.78) [11].
  4. Data analysis and correlation to hydrology (separate system hydrology and wet-weather generation) (WEF FD-6, §3.5.4, §3.5.8–3.5.10, p.6) [15] [37] [10].
  5. Rehabilitation planning and engineering deliverables consistent with Orange Book G1-4.2 and WAC 173‑240 (p.57; p.4; p.15) [22] [40] [43].

3) Flow monitoring strategy and periods
- Planning: Establish scope and goals by project type (I/I, SSO, CMOM), which dictates duration/size and required data accuracy (WEF FD-6, §2.1, p.5; §2.3, p.5; §2.1 detailed, p.126–127) [13] [47] [41].
- Use historical data: WRRF records, pump on–off records, rainfall, and long-term flow data where accurate (WEF FD-6, §2.2, p.126) [41].
- Coverage: Gather data to show flows under dry and wet weather and at different times of day, week, and season (WEF FD-6, §2.1 detailed, p.126) [41].
- Monitoring periods: Determined by project scope/type; durations not prescriptive in cited sources (WEF FD-6, §2.1, p.5; §2.1 detailed, p.126) [13] [41].
- Night flow isolation: Perform low-flow measurements during minimum flow periods for infiltration screening (WEF FD-6, §4.4.8, p.78) [11].
- System management linkage: Track pump run-times and flows routinely; calibrate pump run-time meters at least annually (WEF FD-16, §7.1 Routine Management Activities, p.127) [26].

4) Rainfall correlation and hydrologic context
- Set a rainfall scope for analysis and monitoring (WEF FD-6, §2.11, p.5) [9].
- Analyze wet-weather flow generation in separate systems; apply separate system hydrology concepts (WEF FD-6, §3.5.8, §3.5.4, p.6) [37] [15] and assess wet-weather flow responses (WEF FD-6, §3.5.10, p.6) [10].

5) Smoke testing program and QA/QC
- Method reference: WEF smoke testing guidance (WEF FD-6, §2.6.1, p.3) [2].
- Equipment and setup: Use non-toxic, odorless, non-staining smoke; smoke candles with 3–5 min burn; air blower on manhole; consider sandbagging/plugging to isolate segments and increase defect detection (WEF FD-6, §5.2.4–5.2.7, p.206; §5.2.4 title also p.9) [5] [7].
- Plugs: Pneumatic plugs up to 24 in. (rigid) and ~42 in. (non-rigid); require compressed air; safety hazards are significant—tie off, use taglines, deflate from surface, never stand in front of plugs (WEF FD-6, §5.2.5, p.206) [5].
- QA/QC documentation: Constrain test segments (sandbags/plugs); prepare smoke sketch with setup, crew, date; verify smoke reaches test section; check storm manholes and disregard house smoke when mainline cross-leak indicated; walk entire area; photo/video all leaks; map locations with unique identifiers (address) and photo numbers/descriptions (WEF FD-6, §5.2.7, p.206) [5].

6) Dyed-water flooding and leak quantification
- Purpose/method: Pinpoint inflow/RDII and quantify leaks with field measures (weirs, hydrostatic devices, timed containers) alongside dye flooding; pair with CCTV (WEF FD-6, §4.4.6, p.77) [38].
- Dye and flooding practice: Use biodegradable fluorescent dyes; plug storm sewers, fill to no more than half diameter; monitor downstream sanitary manhole for dye presence/concentration as indicator; supply water via hydrants or tank trucks; caution not to overfill (WEF FD-6, §5.3, FIG 5.11a–b narrative, p.212) [14].
- Public notification: Notify utilities and agencies before dye use due to possible public observations (WEF FD-6, §5.3.1, p.212) [14].
- Safety: Review inflatable plug safety (WEF FD-6, §5.2.5, p.206) [5].

7) CCTV inspection
- Include internal CCTV to document defects and confirm smoke/dye findings (WEF FD-6, §4.4, p.2) [1]; coordinate dye flooding with CCTV (WEF FD-6, §4.4.6, p.77) [38].

8) Data management, monitoring, and reporting
- Monitoring conditions and reporting: Establish monitoring locations, frequency, collection methods, and recordkeeping consistent with EPA NPDES monitoring guidance (2010 EPA PWM, Ch. 8 headings, p.7) [34].
- Plan sets must show flow measuring devices and controls schematically; include instrumentation/control schematics and general operating procedures (WAC 173-240-140(2)(c),(g),(h), p.15) [43].
- Instrumentation/control systems must meet applicable codes and reliability requirements (Orange Book G2-6.3 and G2-6.4 headings; EPA 430-99-74-001 referenced for reliability; p.136; p.89) [23] [16].

9) Safety and confined-space compliance
- Address general wastewater safety hazards including confined spaces per Orange Book G2-7 (headings, p.89) [16].
- Follow smoke/dye plug safety procedures (WEF FD-6, §5.2.5, p.206) [5].

10) Deliverables
- Flow monitoring plan and report showing dry/wet-weather behavior across daily/weekly/seasonal cycles and correlation to rainfall scope (WEF FD-6, §2.1 detailed, p.126; §2.11, p.5) [41] [9].
- SSES field packages: manhole inspection logs; smoke test sketches and photo/video leak log with unique IDs (WEF FD-6, §5.2.7, p.206) [5]; dyed-water test forms (WEF FD-6, FIG 5.11b, p.212) [14]; CCTV logs.
- GIS/Map markup of problem areas and potential relief sewers per WEF planning method (WEF FD-6, §2.3 steps, p.126–127) [41].
- Rehabilitation alternatives and recommendations consistent with Orange Book plan/spec requirements (G1-4.2.2, p.57) [22] and WAC 173-240-040 review standards (p.4) [40].
- Monitoring and reporting framework aligned to NPDES permit conditions (2010 EPA PWM, Ch. 8, p.7) [34].
- O&M integration and system management plan (WEF FD-16, §8.1 Operation Plan, p.6; §9.0 System Management, p.7; §7.1 Routine Management Activities, p.127) [3] [6] [26].

Field execution checklist (abbreviated)
- Pre-field
  - Confirm project scope, monitoring goals, and rainfall scope (WEF FD-6, §2.1, p.5; §2.11, p.5) [13] [9].
  - Collect and verify historical flow, rainfall, and pump on–off records (WEF FD-6, §2.2, p.126) [41].
  - Prepare safety plan per Orange Book G2-7 (p.89) [16].
  - Notify agencies/public for dye testing (WEF FD-6, §5.3.1, p.212) [14].
- Flow monitoring
  - Install recording flow monitors at key locations; verify data capture during dry and wet weather and across diurnal/weekly/seasonal cycles (WEF FD-6, §2.1 detailed, p.126) [41].
  - Read and record pump run-time meters; ensure annual calibration (WEF FD-16, §7.1, p.127) [26].
- Smoke testing
  - Equipment: blower, non-toxic smoke, 3–5 min smoke candles, plugs/sandbags, compressed air (WEF FD-6, §5.2.4–5.2.5, p.206) [5].
  - Safety: tie-offs, taglines, surface deflation, keep personnel out of plug trajectory (WEF FD-6, §5.2.5, p.206) [5].
  - QA/QC docs: smoke sketch (setup, crew, date), verify smoke through section, inspect area, photo/video leaks with IDs; note storm sewer smoke and interpret cross-connection (WEF FD-6, §5.2.7, p.206) [5].
- Dyed-water testing
  - Select biodegradable fluorescent dye; plug downstream storm segment; fill ≤ 1/2 diameter; monitor downstream sanitary manhole (WEF FD-6, p.212) [14].
  - Record timing, volumes, dye observations; use standard dye testing form (WEF FD-6, FIG 5.11b, p.212) [14].
  - Pair with CCTV where feasible; perform leak quantification (WEF FD-6, §4.4.6, p.77) [38].
- Night flow isolation
  - Segment basins; measure minimum flows to assess infiltration (WEF FD-6, §4.4.8, p.78) [11].
- Post-field
  - Correlate flows to rainfall scope; apply system hydrology context (WEF FD-6, §2.11, p.5; §3.5.4, §3.5.8–3.5.10, p.6) [9] [15] [37] [10].
  - Compile deliverables per WAC/Orange Book (WAC 173-240-140, p.15; Orange Book G1-4.2.2, p.57) [43] [22].

Minimum instrumentation and equipment requirements (from cited sources)
- Flow and rainfall monitoring
  - Recording flow monitors at key system locations; integrate rainfall and pump station run-time records; capture dry and wet weather, and day/week/season variability (WEF FD-6, §2.1–2.2, p.126–127) [41].
  - Pump run-time meters recorded regularly and calibrated at least annually (WEF FD-16, §7.1, p.127) [26].
  - Monitoring/reporting framework consistent with NPDES permit conditions (2010 EPA PWM, Ch. 8, p.7) [34].
  - Note: Specific meter model/accuracy not prescribed in cited sections; adhere to Orange Book ICS and code requirements (G2-6.3/6.4 headings, p.136; p.89) [23] [16].
- Smoke testing
  - Non-toxic, odorless, non-staining smoke media (WEF FD-6, §5.2.7, p.206) [5].
  - Smoke candles with 3–5 minute burn time; portable air blower mounted on manhole (WEF FD-6, §5.2.4, p.206) [5].
  - Segment isolation materials: sandbags and pneumatic plugs; compressed air supply; plugs suited to pipe diameter (rigid body up to ~24 in., non-rigid up to ~42 in.) (WEF FD-6, §5.2.5, p.206) [5].
  - Safety gear: plug tie-offs, stout taglines; extension hose for surface deflation; harness for entry if required; comply with confined space protocols (WEF FD-6, §5.2.5, p.206; Orange Book G2-7 headings, p.89) [5] [16].
- Dyed-water testing
  - Biodegradable fluorescent dyes inert to soils; hoses from hydrant or tanker; inflatable plugs/sandbags for storm sewer isolation; fill ≤ 1/2 pipe diameter; observe downstream sanitary manhole (WEF FD-6, p.212) [14].
  - Standard dye testing form for documentation (WEF FD-6, FIG 5.11b, p.212) [14].
- Instrumentation/control plans
  - Include instrumentation, controls, and sampling schematics in plan submittals (WAC 173‑240‑140(2)(g), p.15) [43].
  - Electrical/ICS to comply with NEC/OSHA and EPA reliability classification (Orange Book G2‑6.3/6.4 headings, p.136) [23].
