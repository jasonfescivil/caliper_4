Checklist — required data, minimum span, and available format guidance

1) Historical influent and effluent flows (continuous/time series)  
- Minimum span: at least one full year of current wastewater flow and loading data (Ecology: engineering report minimum; Ecology recommends >1 year when possible) — see WAC 173‑240‑060 (3)(c) / DOE Orange Book guidance (one year minimum; recommend more) [13 p.6; 12 p.49].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown (sources require credible calibrated meter data but do not mandate a file format) [12 p.49].

2) Historical influent and effluent quality (BOD5, TSS, ammonia, nutrients, TKN, chloride, etc.) — laboratory reports and sample metadata  
- Minimum span: at least one full year of representative lab data to justify design parameters; Ecology recommends more than one year where possible [12 p.49; 13 p.6].  
- Laboratory accreditation requirement: use laboratory data obtained from an Ecology‑accredited laboratory (reporting and QA/QC expected) [12 p.49].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

3) Discharge Monitoring Reports (DMRs) and past NPDES/state permit reports, plus permit file materials (fact sheets, previous permits)  
- Minimum span: permit writers commonly review multiple years; guidance cites DMRs and prior permit files as core permit-file information — request all available DMRs (ideally last 3 years when available) and the current and previous permit(s) / fact sheet(s) [11 p.63; 38 p.133; 40 p.115].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

4) Continuous flow / level logs from meters and lift stations (raw time‑series and calibration history)  
- Minimum span: at least one full year of time‑stamped flow/level data; flow data must come from meters with a documented history of proper calibration (Ecology) [12 p.49; 13 p.6].  
- Calibration history: include meter calibration certificates and calibration schedule/history [12 p.49].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

5) Rainfall and meteorological records for the service area (to correlate RDII / wet‑weather response)  
- Minimum span: multi‑year rainfall record to permit normalization and RDII/I&I comparisons (I/I guidance advises analysis using flow vs. rainfall and comparison to prior reports based on equivalent rainfall) — obtain at least the same period as flow records plus prior multi‑year station record where feasible [42 p.1; 48 p.11].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

6) Water‑use / potable water billing and meter records for the service area (to estimate demand and reclaimed water feasibility)  
- Minimum span: data to cover the period used for flow projections (engineering/feasibility should document historical usage and project 20‑year planning; feasibility analysis must demonstrate 20‑year technical/financial capacity) — obtain at least the same period as flow records and data used to support future demand estimates; feasibility analysis / reclaimed water planning requires 20‑year planning information [4 p.47; 10 p.49; 12 p.49].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

7) Septic / on‑site sewage connections and large on‑site system inventory (existing or planned)  
- Minimum span/details: current inventory and map of private and public on‑site systems and any large on‑site sewage systems affecting the service area (WAC requires discussion of existing private and public wells and other sources as related to proposed facilities) — include current status and locations [27 p.1; 16 p.15].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

8) Asset records and as‑built plans (site plans, process flow diagrams, P&IDs, equipment lists)  
- Minimum content: master site plan, process flow diagram, P&IDs, list of design criteria for each major unit process and design data (plans/specs content per WAC and Orange Book) [14 p.57; 16 WAC 173‑240‑140 p.15 items (a)–(h); G1 plans/spec content references in DOE Orange Book] .  
- Minimum span: current (as‑built) documents and any revisions during construction; include any changes that affect design or capacity [16 p.15; 14 p.57].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

9) SCADA / process historian trends and PLC documentation (time‑series trends for key process variables, alarms, setpoints)  
- Minimum span: at least the same continuous period as flow/quality records requested above (Ecology requires instrumentation/control documentation in plans/specs; DOE Orange Book emphasizes maintainability and PLC documentation) — see G2‑6.4 Instrumentation and Control Systems and WAC 173‑240‑140(2)(g) for instrumentation, controls, and sampling schematics [2 p.89; 16 p.15].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

10) Power reliability / power‑event logs, UPS and transfer events, generator run logs (for reliability assessment)  
- Minimum content: records of UPS/backup generator events and recent power interruptions; DOE/Orange Book includes UPS, switching equipment and grounding items in design reliability topics (G2‑6.4 and switchgear topics) [2 p.89].  
- Minimum span: unknown (not specified in provided sources) — obtain whatever logs exist for the same period as flows if possible.  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

11) Infiltration & Inflow (I/I) investigations, SSES, and RDII analyses (past studies and project certifications)  
- Minimum content/span: copies of past I/I analyses, SSES, and prior comparisons; permit conditions may require I/I evaluations and comparisons to prior reports using equivalent rainfall and may require corrective action if I/I increased >15% (see permit requirement example) — request all past I/I reports and the data used to produce them [48 p.11; 42 p.1].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

12) Past engineering reports, feasibility studies, planning documents, cost analyses, and O&M manuals  
- Minimum span: all available historical reports; feasibility analysis must demonstrate 20‑year planning (capital, O&M, revenues) and engineering report must be sufficiently complete for plans/specs [4 p.47; 13 p.6; 16 p.15].  
- O&M manual: for reclaimed water projects an O&M manual must be submitted prior to operation; include manufacturer data, operating instructions, and emergency procedures [3 p.24].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

13) Compliance/inspection records, incident/overflow reports, and corrective‑action documentation (including CMOM / SSO records if any)  
- Minimum span: all available; EPA guidance cites retention of monitoring and overflow records and recommends inclusion of inspection reports and compliance correspondence for permit development [11 p.63; 23 p.201].  
- Record retention: monitoring records must be retained at least 3 years per EPA guidance (subject to state extension) — request at least last 3 years of records [31 p.179].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

14) Laboratory QA/QC and chain‑of‑custody records for key samples used in engineering analyses  
- Minimum span: at least the same period as the water quality data requested above; lab data must be from an Ecology‑accredited lab and include QA/QC to be credible for design [12 p.49].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

15) GIS or plan maps showing locations of sewers, pump stations, force mains, reclaimed water facilities, potable sources (wellhead protection zones), and proposed reuse sites  
- Minimum content: maps required in engineering report and general sewer plan (layout maps, topography, locations of pumping stations, force mains, wells, reservoirs) — see WAC 173‑240‑050 and WAC 173‑240‑060 for map requirements [27 p.1; 13 p.6].  
- Format specified in provided sources: unknown.

How to submit the data (required/expected by reviewing agencies, per provided sources)  
- Provide credible, calibrated flow meter data and Ecology‑accredited laboratory results; ensure documentation of calibration and lab accreditation is included with the data [12 p.49].  
- Include a project location map, service area map, and maps showing potable water supply sources and reclaimed water facility locations when applicable (WAC 173‑219‑210 and WAC 173‑240 series) [21 p.20; 13 p.6].  
- Provide engineering reports and O&M manuals: engineering reports must be sufficiently complete so plans/specs can be developed without substantial changes and O&M manual must be submitted prior to operation for reclaimed water facilities [13 p.6; 3 p.24].  
- Permit/permit‑file documents to include DMRs, compliance inspection reports, and prior engineering reports — these are standard permit‑file items used in permit development and feasibility review [11 p.63; 40 p.115].

Notes / Explicit citations to governing sections (exact section names / page labels from provided context)  
- WAC 173‑240‑060 Engineering report — minimum data requirements including “present and expected future quantity and quality of wastewater” and requirement that report be sufficiently complete so plans/specs can be developed [13 p.6].  
- DOE Orange Book: “The analysis must, at a minimum, include one full year of current wastewater flow and loading data… Ecology recommends using more than one year of data” (Design Standards / Criteria) [12 p.49].  
- WAC 173‑219 (Reclaimed Water): feasibility analysis must demonstrate long‑term technical/management/financial capacity over a 20‑year planning period; engineering report must include maps and potable water coordination per WAC 173‑219‑210/180 (see feasibility & engineering report requirements) [4 p.47; 5 p.17; 21 p.20].  
- WAC 173‑240‑050 General sewer plan — map and service area content requirements (layout, pump stations, sewers, wells) [27 p.1].  
- WAC 173‑240‑140 Plans and specifications — require instrumentation, controls, sampling schematics and general operating procedures in plans/specs [16 p.15].  
- SCADA / Instrumentation and Controls: DOE Orange Book G2‑6.4 Instrument and Control System Reliability / Maintainability (see G2‑6.4 headings) [2 p.89].  
- Permit file and DMR importance: EPA Permit Writers’ Manual (permit file review lists DMRs, current permit, fact sheet, engineering reports as key inputs) [11 p.63; 40 p.115].  
- I/I and rainfall normalization: EPA I/I Guide and permit examples requiring I/I evaluation and comparison by equivalent rainfall (and corrective plans if >15% increase) [42 p.1; 48 p.11].  
- Recordkeeping minimums (retention): EPA guidance sets monitoring record retention at least three years (subject to extension) [31 p.179].  
- O&M manual and operations requirements for reclaimed water: WAC 173‑219‑240 (Operations and maintenance; O&M manual must be submitted prior to operation) [3 p.24].

Limitations / format note (based only on provided sources)
