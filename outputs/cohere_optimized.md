In Washington State, engineering reports for domestic wastewater facilities must adhere to the following **G1 requirements** as synthesized from authoritative sources:

### 1. **Definition & Purpose**
   - **Comprehensive Analysis**: Engineering reports must thoroughly examine engineering/administrative aspects of a project [4,25], providing sufficient detail to develop plans/specifications without substantial changes [4,9,17,18,20,22,30].
   - **Basis for Design**: Serve as the technical foundation for facility design, including treatment processes, hydraulic calculations, and site-specific evaluations [4,9,17,18,20,22].

### 2. **Submittal Requirements**
   - **Mandatory Submission**: Reports must be submitted to the Washington State Department of Ecology (Ecology) for review/approval before construction [1,4,9,12,15,17,18,19,20,21,22,25,26,30,31].
   - **Timing**: Submit at least 60 days before desired approval [1,15] (or 90 days for federally funded projects [13]).
   - **Draft & Final Copies**: Ecology accepts electronic drafts [17] and requires final copies signed/sealed by a licensed Washington PE [9,13,14,16,17,22,25].

### 3. **Content Requirements**
   - **Project Identification**:  
     - Owner/authorized representative contact info [9,17,18,20].  
     - Project description, location maps, and service area boundaries [9,15,17,18,20,22].  
   - **Wastewater Characterization**:  
     - Present/future wastewater quantity, quality (BOD₅, TSS), and industrial waste impacts [9,15,17,18,20,22].  
   - **Treatment Design**:  
     - Proposed treatment processes, design criteria, and justification [9,15,17,18,20,22].  
     - Basic design data/sizing calculations for all treatment units [17,18,20].  
   - **Site Evaluation**:  
     - Alternative site analysis, advantages/disadvantages [17,18,20].  
     - Floodplain/environmental impact considerations [9,15,17,18,20,22,32].  
   - **Cost Analysis**:  
     - Capital and O&M cost estimates over the project life [9,15,17,18,20,22].  
   - **Compliance Statements**:  
     - SEPA/NEPA/SERP compliance [9,15,17,18,20,22,32].  
     - Adherence to water quality standards (Chapters 90.48, 90.54 RCW) [1,4,12,15].  

### 4. **Special Cases**
   - **Reclaimed Water Projects**:  
     - Coordinate with wastewater/LOSS system reports [7,10]; include water quality assessments for beneficial uses [10,32].  
     - Address pipe labeling (Chapter 51-56 WAC) and cross-connection prevention [23].  
   - **CSO Reduction Plans**:  
     - Include sediment/discharge monitoring, environmental baseline studies, and source control strategies [6].  
   - **Industrial Wastewater**:  
     - Tailored requirements for waste characterization, pretreatment, and sludge disposal [12,19].  

### 5. **Professional Standards**
   - **PE Supervision**: Reports must be prepared under a Washington-licensed Professional Engineer [9,13,14,16,17,22,25].  
   - **Seal/Signature**: Final reports require a PE seal/signature [9,13,14,16,17,22].  

### 6. **Review Process**
   - **Ecology Approval**: Regional offices review for regulatory compliance [1,4,9,12,15,17,18,19,20,21,22,25,26,30,31].  
   - **Review Timeline**: Ecology aims to complete preliminary reviews within 60–90 days [1,13,15], but complexity may extend timelines [1].  
   - **Coordination**: Early consultation with Ecology regional staff is highly recommended [1].  

### 7. **Exceptions**
   - **Sewer Line Extensions**: Reports may be waived if conforming to an approved general sewer plan [1,4,30].  
   - **Low-Capacity Systems**: Subsurface disposal systems require public ownership and loading limits [26].  

### Key Citations:
- **WAC 173-240-060** [12,19,20,21,22]: Core engineering report requirements.  
- **DOE Orange Book (Ch. G1)** [1,4,6,9,15,17,18]: Detailed content guidelines.  
- **Reclaimed Water Manual (Ch. 5)** [7,10,13,14,23,24,32]: Additional criteria for reuse projects.  

These requirements ensure facilities protect public health and the environment while meeting state/federal regulations.
